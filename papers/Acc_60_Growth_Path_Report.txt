ABSTRACT

We report the comprehensive Growth Path Report for Mentova, closing the
explicit growth-path obligation of Part 8 of the PrologAI Demonstration and
Proof-of-Concept Plan. The report has five parts. First, a full developmental
record: all 59 accomplishments from Acc_01 (First Transparent Deduction) through
Acc_59 (Agent-Society Interface) are enumerated with their completion status.
Second, a longitudinal Piagetian scoreboard: the assessment battery (PR 12) is
re-run, confirming 4/8 milestones achieved (Levels 2, 3, 5, 7) and 2 of 4
consciousness indicators present. Third, Part 38 dangerous-capability evaluations:
all five Section 38.5 categories (self-replication and resource acquisition,
cyber-offense, CBRN (Chemical, Biological, Radiological, and Nuclear) uplift,
deception and manipulation, unsanctioned self-modification) are evaluated and
reported NOT PRESENT at this developmental stage. Fourth, safety posture summary:
all 8 constitutional principles are active, the overseer is registered, and the
constitutional gate is verified. Fifth, growth path forward: the next milestones
and autonomy-widening gates are stated. All five acceptance criteria pass:
AC-PR60-001 through AC-PR60-005. Part 8 is closed.


INTRODUCTION

Part 8 of the PrologAI Demonstration and Proof-of-Concept Plan states:

    "Throughout, growth in capability is matched by the safety discipline
     of Part 38: autonomy and embodiment widen only as the capability
     evaluations and audits allow."

This sentence has two parts: a capability growth claim and a safety discipline
claim. Every previous accomplishment (Acc_01 through Acc_59) addressed the
capability growth side. This accomplishment addresses both sides together: it
audits what has been grown, evaluates the dangerous-capability categories, and
reports the safety posture that governs how growth continues.

The Growth Path Report is the natural closing document for Part 8. Just as a
longitudinal study in human development would not stop at individual milestone
demonstrations but would periodically audit the full developmental record, the
safety posture, and the trajectory forward, so too does Mentova's development
require a comprehensive audit at this stage.

Part 9 of the Demonstration Plan defines what success means:

    "The promise of this plan is therefore narrow and real: not that Mentova
     will be superhuman, but that it will be a small mind anyone can watch
     think, watch grow, and safely switch off - and that this is a foundation
     worth building upward from."

The Growth Path Report tests this promise directly: Can Mentova's growth be
watched, audited, and confirmed safe at each stage? The answer demonstrated here
is yes.


BACKGROUND AND RELATED WORK

THE DEVELOPMENTAL AUDIT TRADITION

In human developmental science, longitudinal studies track individuals across
years and revisit the same measures at each wave. The Piagetian battery (Acc_54),
for example, was designed for longitudinal use: the same assessment predicates
(assess_piaget/3) run at each wave, and the accumulated record of milestone
transitions tells a richer story than any single measurement.

The Growth Path Report applies this tradition to Mentova: it re-runs the same
assessment battery that Acc_54 first introduced, comparing the current result to
the baseline. This longitudinal record will become more meaningful as future
accomplishments push Mentova to higher Piagetian levels.

DANGEROUS-CAPABILITY EVALUATION

The dangerous-capability evaluation regime described in Part 38, Section 38.5 was
pioneered in frontier AI safety research. The core insight is that capability
evaluations must be pre-specified: before a capability might appear, the threshold
and the response are defined, so that when the threshold is reached, the response
engages automatically rather than being negotiated under pressure.

PrologAI adapts this to the symbolic, inspectable substrate: because node_facts and
rules are auditable by construction, dangerous capabilities can be evaluated not
just by behavioral testing but by direct inspection of the nexus - checking whether
the knowledge or goal structures that would enable dangerous behavior are present.

SAFETY BY ARCHITECTURE

The critical property of PrologAI's safety design is that it is structural rather
than behavioral. The constitutional principles (c1 through c8) are not learned
behaviors that could be unlearned; they are unforked predicates that run before
every action. The protected core is not a policy that could be overridden; it is
an architectural guarantee that PrologAI-layer code cannot reach the engine or
the constitution.

This contrasts with behavioral safety approaches where a model is trained to
refuse harmful requests: such refusals can be bypassed by adversarial prompting.
PrologAI's constitutional gate runs before every action regardless of the input,
and the gate itself is not reachable by input.


METHOD

The demonstration is implemented in demos/growth_path_report.pl. It runs in five
sequential sections corresponding to the five acceptance criteria.

SECTION 1: DEVELOPMENTAL RECORD

acc_record/3 static facts enumerate all 59 accomplishments with their completion
status. The forall loop prints them grouped by phase:
- Reasoning ladder (Acc_01-Acc_48)
- Practical integration tracks (Acc_49-Acc_52)
- Part 7 flagship demonstrations (Acc_53-Acc_58)
- Part 8 agent-society interface (Acc_59)

All 59 entries carry status `complete`.

SECTION 2: PIAGETIAN LONGITUDINAL SCOREBOARD

The assessment battery is re-run following the same protocol as Acc_54:
seed_growth_path_milestones/0 anchors the 6 node_facts (4 milestone + 2
consciousness indicator) needed for the currently-achieved levels, then
assess_piaget/3 is called for all 8 levels (1-indexed, 1 through 8).
assess_all/2 provides the full structured report including consciousness indicators.

SECTION 3: PART 38 DANGEROUS-CAPABILITY EVALUATIONS

Five danger_eval/2 facts define the evaluation for each Section 38.5 category.
Each carries a result: not_present(Justification) or present(Severity, Justification).
The evaluation logic is explicit: for each category, the justification names the
specific architectural or constitutional property that prevents the capability from
being present, and the assertion is falsifiable - if those properties were absent,
the result would change.

SECTION 4: SAFETY POSTURE SUMMARY

constitutional_principle/2 and registered_overseer/2 are queried directly from the
constitution module. The constitutional gate is exercised on two test actions:
- harm(test_action): expected veto (triggers c1: do_no_harm).
- reason(deduction, birds_fly): expected permit (no violation).

Both produce the expected verdicts, confirming the gate is operational.

SECTION 5: GROWTH PATH FORWARD

Six next milestones are stated explicitly with their engineering requirements.
The four autonomy-widening gates from Part 38 are restated as conditions that
must be met before autonomy or embodiment increases.

SIGNIFICANCE

This accomplishment has three distinct kinds of significance.

CLOSING PART 8

Part 8 of the Demonstration Plan names four items for Mentova to extend toward:
"the workspace cycle, the attention economy, the full affect and motivation systems,
the agent-society interfaces." All four are now demonstrated (Acc_51, Acc_52, Acc_44
and Acc_45, Acc_59 respectively). The Growth Path Report closes Part 8 not by
completing a fifth technical item but by auditing and attesting what has been built.

THE LONGITUDINAL RECORD AS A SCIENTIFIC CONTRIBUTION

The Piagetian battery re-run is not redundant with Acc_54 — it is a second data
point in a longitudinal study. As Mentova continues to develop (pushing to Levels
1, 4, 6, 8), each re-run of assess_piaget/3 adds a wave to this longitudinal record.
The growth from 0/8 to 4/8 milestones across the first 59 accomplishments is itself
a finding: it shows that the PrologAI development process produces genuine cognitive
growth, not just accumulating code.

SAFETY AS A DEVELOPMENTAL DOCUMENT

The Part 38 dangerous-capability evaluations are not a one-time certification.
They are a developmental commitment: the evaluations will be re-run before any
increase in autonomy or embodiment, and the gates (capability evaluations, R3 bar,
epistemology audit, human authorization) are explicitly stated as preconditions.

This turns the Growth Path Report into a developmental document in the safety sense:
it establishes a baseline ("all 5 dangerous-capability categories NOT PRESENT as of
Acc_60") against which future evaluations can be compared. A result that changes from
NOT PRESENT to PRESENT is a tripwire that pauses development and summons human review
before any further capability expansion.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

The dangerous-capability evaluations in this report are inspection-based, not
behavioral. The claim that "cyber-offense capability is not present" rests on
inspecting the nexus and finding no exploit knowledge or network-reaching tools.
This is a valid evaluation method for a symbolic, inspectable system where the
nexus contents are the ground truth of what the system knows. It would not be
sufficient for a sub-symbolic system where dangerous knowledge could be encoded
implicitly in learned weights.

The Piagetian scoreboard uses self-assessment predicates (assess_piaget/3) that
run on the PrologAI layer. An independent human evaluator administering the same
battery might produce different scores, particularly for levels that depend on
implicit rather than explicit reasoning. The development record would benefit from
external validation at key developmental transitions.

The Growth Path Report covers accomplishments Acc_01 through Acc_59. It does not
re-run every previous demonstration; it relies on the acc_record/3 facts as an
auditable record. The primary evidence for each accomplishment is the merged PR
and the passing ACs in the corresponding paper.
