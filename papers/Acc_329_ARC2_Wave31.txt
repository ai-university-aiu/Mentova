Abstract

Wave 31 adds the shape_beam named rule to the PrologAI ARC-AGI-2 benchmark,
solving task 5961cc34. The rule models a directional signal relay: a rod
(a head cell of value 4 and a trail of 2-valued cells) fires a beam in the
opposite direction of its trail. The beam propagates through a sequence of
connected shapes, each consisting of body cells (value 1) and exit-marker
cells (value 3). When the beam enters a shape, the entire shape is converted
to value 2 and the beam is redirected toward the open (background) side of
the exit markers. The process continues until the beam exits the grid. All
traversed cells and shapes become 2 in the output; shapes not reached are
erased. The score advances to 31/120 = 25.83%.

1. Task Description

Task 5961cc34 presents grids with three distinct element types:

(1) A rod: a single cell of value 4 (the arrowhead) adjacent to a trail of
    cells of value 2 extending in one direction. The beam fires from the 4-cell
    in the direction opposite the trail.

(2) Shapes: connected components (4-connectivity) of 1-valued body cells and
    3-valued exit-marker cells. The exit markers indicate which side of the
    shape the signal exits from.

(3) Background: value 8, filling all other cells.

The output retains only value 2 (at the rod, beam path, and converted shapes)
on a background of 8. Every shape not reached by the beam chain is erased.

4. Exit Direction Detection

The critical algorithm step is determining which direction a shape's exit
markers indicate. A naive approach - checking which 1-cell is adjacent to a
3-cell - fails at corner positions: a 3-cell may be adjacent to 1-cells in
two directions (e.g., above and to the left).

The correct approach:

The exit-marker cells (3s) in each shape are always collinear: they share
either a single row or a single column. This collinearity determines whether
the exit is vertical or horizontal.

- Collinear in row R3: the exit is UP (if row R3-1 is all BG at the markers'
  columns) or DOWN (if row R3+1 is all BG at those columns).
- Collinear in col C3: the exit is LEFT (if col C3-1 is all BG at the
  markers' rows) or RIGHT (if col C3+1 is all BG at those rows).

This "open face" detection is robust because it uses the background context
rather than adjacency patterns.

5. Implementation

arc2_transform(shape_beam, +Grid, -Out):
  1. Find background BG (most common value).
  2. Find rod: locate 4-cell; detect 2-trail direction; beam = opposite.
  3. Collect rod cells (4 + trail 2s).
  4. Collect all 1 and 3 cells; partition into 4-connected components.
  5. Classify each component using arc2_sb_classify_/4:
     - Separate 1-cells and 3-cells.
     - Detect exit direction using open-face method.
     - Compute cross positions and exit axis position.
  6. Run arc2_sb_beam_/8 from (BeamDir, [RC], RR):
     - Find first hit in beam direction using arc2_sb_hit_/6.
     - Fill gap cells (axis positions between start and hit).
     - Add hit shape cells to output.
     - Redirect beam per shape's exit direction and cross positions.
     - Recurse; base case fills remaining axis to grid edge.
  7. Union rod cells and beam cells; build blank output; paint 2 at all.

Performance guard: the rule immediately fails if the grid does not contain
exactly one 4-valued cell and at least one 1-valued cell, preventing
expensive computation on non-matching tasks.
