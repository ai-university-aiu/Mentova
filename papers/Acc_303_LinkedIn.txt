Mentova just solved its fifth ARC-AGI-2 evaluation task using a new glass-box rule called stub_rank_fill.

The rule: the grid contains vertical bars and short color stubs at the column tops. A vertical bar is a column segment [V, B, B, ..., B, V] - endpoint color V at top and bottom, body color B in between. A stub of height N at a column with color V is an instruction: "find all bars with endpoint color V, sort by body length descending, and fill the body of the Nth one with V."

The height of the stub IS the rank. Stub height 1 selects the longest matching bar. Stub height 2 selects the second longest. Multiple stubs for different colors can appear simultaneously; each selects its own bar independently.

What makes this pattern elegant is that the encoding is purely geometric. The grid carries its own instruction manual: the stub lengths define the answer. No color legend, no external map needed. Pure structure-as-symbol.
