ABSTRACT

Wave 29 adds the diag_beam named rule to the PrologAI ARC-AGI-2 solver,
solving task db695cfb and advancing the score from 28/120 = 23.33% to

The rule identifies pairs of 1-cells that lie exactly on a 45-degree diagonal
from each other, fires a diagonal beam between each pair, fills intermediate
background cells on the beam with 1, and when the beam strikes an existing
6-cell, reflects two perpendicular 45-degree rays that fill background cells
with 6 until the grid boundary or another existing 6 is reached.

TASK DESCRIPTION

Task db695cfb presents grids whose cells are one of three values: a background
color, 1, and 6. Some 1-cells are placed at positions that are diagonally
aligned: the row distance and column distance to another 1-cell are equal and
non-zero. These aligned pairs define 45-degree beam axes. Between the two 1s,
some grids also place 6-cells directly on the beam axis. The output fills the
beam path with 1s (skipping existing 6-cells, which stay) and radiates pairs
of perpendicular 6-trails from every 6-cell hit by the beam.

RULE: DIAG-BEAM

Step 1 - Guard verification.

Read the background color as Grid[0][0]. Verify that every non-background
cell in the grid has value exactly 1 or 6. Collect all 1-cell positions into
Ones and all 6-cell positions into Sixes.

Step 2 - Find diagonal pairs.

For every unordered pair (P1, P2) of positions in Ones where:
  |row(P1) - row(P2)| = |col(P1) - col(P2)|  and  |row(P1) - row(P2)| > 0
the pair defines a 45-degree diagonal beam. Collect all such pairs.

Step 3 - Fire each beam.

For each pair (R1,C1)-(R2,C2), compute the unit step direction:
  DR = sign(R2 - R1),  DC = sign(C2 - C1).
March from (R1+DR, C1+DC) toward (R2, C2) one step at a time:
  - If the current cell matches a position in the original Sixes list
    (an existing 6-obstacle), do not modify the cell. Instead, fire two
    perpendicular rays from it and continue marching.
  - Otherwise, fill the current cell with 1.
Stop when the march reaches (R2, C2), which is a 1-cell already.

Step 4 - Perpendicular bounce rays.

When the beam hits a 6-obstacle at (R, C) with beam direction (DR, DC),
fire two rays in the perpendicular directions (DR, -DC) and (-DR, DC).
Each ray advances one step at a time from the obstacle:
  - Stop if the next cell is outside the grid.
  - Stop if the next cell is in the original Sixes list (existing 6 blocks).
  - Otherwise fill the cell with 6 and advance.
Rays use only the original Sixes; newly placed 6-fills do not block later rays.

Step 5 - Thread the grid.

Apply all beams in sequence using foldl, threading the modified grid through
each beam step so each beam sees all prior modifications.

IMPLEMENTATION

Key predicates:

arc2_transform(diag_beam, Grid, Out)
    Top-level guard and driver. Verifies background structure, collects
    Ones and Sixes, finds diagonal pairs, and calls foldl over the pairs.

arc2_db_pair_(Sixes, NRows, NCols, Pair, G0, G1)
    Unpacks one pair, computes DR and DC with if-then-else (R2>R1 -> 1 ; -1),
    starts one step past the source, and invokes arc2_db_march_.

arc2_db_march_(R, C, DR, DC, R2, C2, Sixes, NRows, NCols, G0, G1)
    Base case: R=R2, C=C2 - destination reached, unify G=G1.
    Recursive case: check memberchk(R-C, Sixes). If obstacle, bounce;
    otherwise fill with 1. Advance NR=R+DR, NC=C+DC and recur.

arc2_db_bounce_(R, C, DR, DC, Sixes, NRows, NCols, G0, G1)
    Fires arc2_db_ray_ twice: with (DR, -DC) and (-DR, DC).

arc2_db_ray_(R, C, DR, DC, Sixes, NRows, NCols, G0, G1)
    Extends one step; stops at boundary (NR<0 or >=NRows etc.), stops at
    memberchk(NR-NC, Sixes), or fills with 6 and recurses.
