ABSTRACT

We report Rung 26 of Mentova's 48-rung reasoning ladder: scientific reasoning.
Mentova implements a hypothesis discovery loop: propose a hypothesis, test it against
the observation table, and score it. The hypothesis "most canary observations show
yellow colour" is proposed, tested against 50 observations (47 yellow, 3 green),
and scored 0.94 — graded confirmed. Pass criterion met: hypothesis proposed, tested,
and scored.


INTRODUCTION

Scientific reasoning involves forming a hypothesis, collecting or consulting evidence,
and evaluating the hypothesis against that evidence. Rung 26 implements this discovery
loop in Mentova using the Small-World observation table.


BACKGROUND

The hypothetico-deductive method (Popper, 1934) is the canonical scientific method:
propose a hypothesis, deduce predictions, test predictions against observation.
Mentova's implementation uses inductive scoring (evidence coverage) rather than
Popperian falsification, which is appropriate for the small-observation-table setting.


METHOD

A new module, src/mentova/scientific.pl, implements:

    mentova_scientific(+Query, -Result, -Justification)

Hypothesis templates: dominant_colour, rarely_flies, mostly_red, always_swims.
Test: aggregate observation counts and compute coverage score.
Grade: confirmed (≥0.8), supported (≥0.6), weakly_supported (≥0.4), not_supported (<0.4).


DEMONSTRATION

    ?- mentova_query(scientific, test_hypothesis(dominant_colour(canary,yellow)), R).
    R = answer(result(0.94, confirmed, evidence(canary,yellow,47,of(50))),
               just(scientific_test(..., score(0.94), grade(confirmed))))

    47/50 = 0.94. Grade: confirmed.


RESULTS

Hypothesis scored 0.94 (confirmed). Evidence: 47 yellow observations out of 50.
Pass criterion met: hypothesis proposed, tested, and scored.


SIGNIFICANCE

Scientific reasoning closes the loop between knowledge acquisition (induction) and
knowledge evaluation (testing). It is the mechanism by which Mentova's knowledge
base can be expanded and validated empirically.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

The scoring function is a simple coverage ratio, not a likelihood ratio or Bayesian
posterior. A statistically rigorous test would use chi-squared or Fisher's exact test.
