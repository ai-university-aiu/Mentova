ABSTRACT

We report Rung 39 of Mentova's 48-rung reasoning ladder: teleological reasoning.
Mentova answers "what is X for?" by consulting a purpose knowledge base with
teleological chains. The heart pumps blood, serving the circulatory system,
enabling oxygen delivery, enabling cell survival. A hammer drives nails, serving
construction, enabling joining materials, enabling structure building.
Pass criterion met: purpose and teleological chain returned for any artifact or organ.


INTRODUCTION

Teleological reasoning addresses final causes: what is something for? Aristotle
identified final cause as one of his four causes. In cognitive science, teleological
reasoning is how children (and adults) understand artifacts and biological structures.


BACKGROUND

Teleological knowledge representation (Chandrasekaran and Josephson, 2000) captures
functional hierarchies. The purpose of a part is what role it plays in a larger system.


METHOD

A new module, src/mentova/teleological.pl, implements:

    mentova_teleological(+Query, -Result, -Justification)

Twelve entities with purposes and three-level chains. Queries: purpose_of,
ultimate_goal, what_serves, why.


DEMONSTRATION

    ?- mentova_query(teleological, purpose_of(heart), R).
    R = answer(purpose(heart, pump_blood,
                       serves(circulatory_system, enables(oxygen_delivery, enables(cell_survival)))),
               ...)

    ?- mentova_query(teleological, ultimate_goal(roots), R).
    R = answer(ultimate(roots, plant_growth), ...)


RESULTS

Heart: pump_blood → circulatory_system → oxygen_delivery → cell_survival.
Roots: absorb_water_and_nutrients → plant_growth (ultimate). Pass criterion met.


SIGNIFICANCE

Teleological reasoning is essential for biology, engineering design, and natural
language understanding. "What is this for?" is one of the most fundamental questions.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

Purposes are stored explicitly. Functional decomposition from physical structure
(reverse engineering) is not supported.


REPRODUCIBILITY

Requirements: SWI-Prolog 9.0.4 or later.

  git clone https://github.com/ai-university-aiu/Mentova.git
  cd Mentova
  swipl -l src/mentova/mentova.pl \
        -g "mentova_boot, mentova_query(teleological, purpose_of(heart), R), write(R), nl" \
        -t halt
