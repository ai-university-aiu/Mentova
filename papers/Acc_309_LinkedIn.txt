Mentova just solved ARC-AGI-2 task 8e5c0c38, advancing to 11 out of 120 = 9.17%.

The rule is called sym_restore and the concept is elegant.

Each non-background colour group in the input grid is almost left-right
symmetric but has a handful of cells that break the mirror.  The task is
to identify and remove those cells, restoring exact vertical symmetry for
every colour independently.

The algorithm is straightforward:

For each colour group, scan every possible vertical axis (the column value
can be integer or half-integer - both are handled by doubling the axis and
working in integers).  For each axis count the orphan cells - cells whose
mirror position is absent from the group.  Pick the axis with the fewest
orphans, then remove those cells by setting them to background.

That is the entire rule.  No heuristics, no special cases.

On the test case there are three colour groups:
- Colour 6 (58 cells): axis at col 9.5, 12 orphans removed.
- Colour 3 (8 cells): axis at col 18.0, 2 orphans removed.
- Colour 4 (22 cells): axis at col 6.0, 3 orphans removed.

17 cells removed in total.  The expected output matches exactly.

One subtle point: the axis can be a half-integer.  Colour 6's axis at 9.5
means the shape is symmetric about a point between columns 9 and 10, with
no central column.  The training pair 1 colour-2 group also uses a
half-integer axis at 16.5.  The doubling trick handles this uniformly:
axis 9.5 becomes Ax2 = 19, and the mirror of column C is column 19 - C.
