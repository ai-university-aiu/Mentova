Accomplishment 87: Shape Pack - Normalized Shape Extraction and D4 Orbit Reasoning

D. R. Dison, Founder of AIU, Creator and Owner of PrologAI and Mentova, ORCID 0009-0001-9246-5758, linkedin.com/in/d-r-dison/

Abstract

PrologAI now includes the shape pack (module shape, prefix sh_), a set of
14 predicates for working with normalized cell patterns extracted from
grids. A shape is a sorted list of r(R,C) cells translated so that the
minimum row and minimum column are both 0. The pack provides creation,
comparison, spatial transformation (rotation, reflection), D4 orbit
enumeration, canonical form computation, and grid placement for shapes.
The shape pack is the 65th work package in PrologAI and Mentova's 87th
accomplishment.

Introduction

The grid pack gives Mentova raw cell access. The color pack gives it color
as a first-class concept. The shape pack gives it the concept of a spatial
pattern independent of position and color.

This distinction is fundamental. A T-shaped cluster of cells is the same
T-shape whether it appears in the top-left corner of a grid or the
bottom-right. It is the same T-shape whether its cells are colored red or
blue. Position and color are properties of a placed instance of a shape.
The shape itself is the abstract pattern.

By normalizing cell sets to the origin, the shape pack creates a
representation where two instances of the same pattern always produce the
same data structure. This enables reliable equality testing, pattern
matching, and induction.

Design

A shape is a sorted list of r(R,C) cells satisfying two invariants:
(1) min(R) = 0 and min(C) = 0, so the shape touches the origin.
(2) The list is sorted in standard Prolog term order, so two identical
patterns always produce identical lists regardless of the order in which
cells were discovered.

Normalization is performed by sh_from_cells/2, which subtracts the minimum
row and column from all cells and then sorts. This is O(N log N) for N cells.

sh_from_grid/3 combines findall over grid cells with sh_from_cells, giving
a direct path from a grid and a color to a normalized shape.

Spatial Transformations

The shape pack implements all four generators needed to produce the D4
(dihedral group of the square) orbit:

sh_rotate90/2 applies the 90-degree clockwise rotation r(R,C) -> r(C, MaxR-R)
and renormalizes.

sh_reflect_h/2 applies the horizontal (left-right) flip r(R,C) -> r(R, MaxC-C)
and renormalizes.

sh_reflect_v/2 applies the vertical (top-bottom) flip r(R,C) -> r(MaxR-R, C)
and renormalizes.

sh_translate/4 applies a (DR, DC) offset without renormalizing - this is the
operation needed to place a normalized shape at a specific grid position.

D4 Orbit and Canonical Form

sh_orbit/2 computes all distinct D4 orientations of a shape. It generates
4 rotations of the shape and 4 rotations of its horizontal reflection, then
removes duplicates. The result has 1 element (fully symmetric), 2 elements
(symmetric under 180-degree rotation but not reflection), 4 elements
(one axis of symmetry), or 8 elements (asymmetric).

sh_canonical/2 returns the lex-min element of the orbit. Two shapes have
the same canonical form if and only if they are D4-equivalent.

sh_equivalent/2 tests D4 equivalence via canonical form comparison. This
is the key predicate for shape-based induction: if two grids contain the
same shape type in different orientations, sh_equivalent confirms this.

Grid Placement

sh_to_grid/6 places a shape on a grid at a given (DR, DC) offset with a
given color. Combined with sh_translate, sh_rotate90, and sh_reflect_h, this
enables composing the full ARC-AGI-2 placement pipeline: extract a shape,
find the right orientation, compute the placement offset, paint.

Conclusion

The shape pack closes the gap between raw cell access (grid pack) and
abstract pattern reasoning. Together with the color, symmetry, path, and
frame packs, Mentova now has a complete first-order vocabulary for grid
perception: what cells are there, what color they are, how they are
connected, whether they form borders, and what abstract patterns they
represent. The next packs build compositional reasoning on this vocabulary.
