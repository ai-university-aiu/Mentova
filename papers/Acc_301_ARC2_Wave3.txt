WHAT WAS BUILT

New rule: arm_endpoint_ray

The arm_endpoint_ray rule is implemented in arc_benchmark_2.pl via six new
predicates:

arc2_replace_idx_/4
  Replaces the element at a 0-based index in a list, returning a new list.
  Used as the building block for arc2_set_cell_/5.

arc2_set_cell_/5
  Returns a new grid with a single cell replaced by a given value. Uses
  arc2_replace_idx_/4 twice: once to update the target row, once to update
  the row within the grid.

arc2_diag_nbr_cnt_/6
  Counts same-color diagonal neighbors in the main-diagonal direction
  (NMain = count of matches at (R-1,C-1) and (R+1,C+1)) and the
  anti-diagonal direction (NAnti = count of matches at (R-1,C+1) and
  (R+1,C-1)).

arc2_arm_endpoints_/2
  Finds all valid arm endpoints: non-(0,1) cells that have exactly one
  same-color diagonal neighbor, and that neighbor lies entirely in one
  diagonal direction (NMain=1 and NAnti=0, or NMain=0 and NAnti=1). Cells
  where NMain+NAnti=0 are isolated and not endpoints. Cells where
  NMain+NAnti=2 with both directions occupied are junction/corner cells and
  not endpoints. Only cells with exactly one same-color direction match are
  true arm endpoints.

arc2_shoot_ray_/9
  Steps from a starting cell (R,C) in direction (DR,DC), filling each
  consecutive background-1 cell with the arm color V. Stops when the next
  step would be out of bounds or would land on a non-1 cell (either a
  background-0 cell or a special cell that blocks the ray).

arc2_apply_arm_rays_/5
  Applies the perpendicular ray from each arm endpoint in sequence. For an
  endpoint with Dir=anti (arm is main-diagonal), shoots rays in directions
  (+1,-1) and (-1,+1). For an endpoint with Dir=main (arm is anti-diagonal),
  shoots rays in directions (+1,+1) and (-1,-1).


Algorithm description:

The input is a checkerboard of alternating 0s and 1s, with special non-bg
cells embedded at positions where the background would be 1 (i.e., at
positions where (R+C) is even, if 1-cells are at even parity).

Two types of special cells appear:
1. Diamond outline cells: form a closed rotated-square boundary. Each
   diamond cell connects to same-color cells in BOTH diagonal directions
   (main-diagonal and anti-diagonal), making it either a midpoint of a
   segment or a junction point.
2. Arm segment cells: form short linear segments in one diagonal direction
   only. Each cell in an arm connects to same-color cells in exactly ONE
   diagonal direction.

The rule identifies arm endpoints (cells at the ends of arm segments) and
shoots rays perpendicular to the arm's diagonal direction. Each ray fills
background-1 cells with the arm color until reaching a grid boundary or a
blocking non-bg cell (typically a diamond cell on the near side, or another
arm cell on the far side).

The critical distinction between arm endpoints and diamond/junction cells:
a valid endpoint has same-color diagonal neighbors in exactly ONE direction
(NMain XOR NAnti = 1). Diamond corner cells have neighbors in BOTH
directions (NMain=1 AND NAnti=1), so they are junctions and are not
endpoints. Diamond edge cells that look like midpoints have two neighbors
in the SAME direction (e.g., NAnti=2), so they are midpoints and are not
endpoints. This structural test cleanly separates arm endpoints from all
diamond cells without needing any explicit diamond detection logic.

Arm direction and ray direction:
- Arm cells connected via main-diagonal (r-c=const) have Dir=anti. Their
  perpendicular rays run along anti-diagonals (r+c=const), shot in (+1,-1)
  and (-1,+1) directions.
- Arm cells connected via anti-diagonal (r+c=const) have Dir=main. Their
  perpendicular rays run along main diagonals (r-c=const), shot in (+1,+1)
  and (-1,-1) directions.

Induction guard for arm_endpoint_ray:
A fast 2x2 checkerboard check is applied to the first training input
before any expensive computation: (0,0) and (1,1) must be equal; (0,1)
and (1,0) must be equal; (0,0) and (0,1) must differ. This rejects all
non-checkerboard tasks in O(1) time.

SIGNIFICANCE

Wave 3 demonstrates that Mentova's symbolic reasoning engine can detect
and exploit geometric structure in structured-background grids (checkerboard
patterns). The arm_endpoint_ray rule introduces three new capabilities:

1. Cell mutation in-place. The arc2_set_cell_/5 predicate and
   arc2_replace_idx_/4 enable efficient single-cell updates to a grid
   represented as a list of lists. This is the first time the benchmark
   runner performs targeted cell modification rather than wholesale grid
   replacement, enabling multi-step ray accumulation.

2. Structural endpoint detection. The arc2_diag_nbr_cnt_/6 and
   arc2_arm_endpoints_/2 predicates introduce a principled test for arm
   endpoints that cleanly separates them from diamond junction cells and
   midpoints, using only local neighbor counts rather than global shape
   detection. This is a minimal and robust structural classifier.

3. Multi-construct generalization. The test case has three separate
   arm-diamond constructs with different colors and orientations. The rule
   handles all three simultaneously without task-specific configuration,
   demonstrating that the endpoint detection and ray-shooting algorithm
   generalizes across constructs of varying complexity.

The key architectural lesson from this wave: ARC-AGI-2 tasks embed
semantically meaningful geometric relations in checkerboard backgrounds.
The checkerboard acts as a "carrier medium" that distinguishes fg-1 cells
(the meaningful signal positions) from fg-0 cells (the gaps). Rules that
operate only on fg-1 cells and interpret diagonal cell groups as line
segments can extract the geometric relationships encoded in the task.

The arm_endpoint_ray rule also introduces the general pattern of
"endpoint detection + perpendicular projection," which is likely to appear
in multiple ARC-AGI-2 tasks with different surface forms. Future waves
should look for this pattern with non-checkerboard backgrounds or with
different projection rules (e.g., wrap-around projection, bounded
projection, multi-color projection).
