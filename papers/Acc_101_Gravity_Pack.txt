Accomplishment 101: Directional Gravity and Settling - The Gravity Pack

Date: 2026-06-25

Abstract

Mentova's cognitive substrate gains its 79th work package: the gravity pack (gv_* predicate family, Layer 60). This pack implements physics-like directional settling of grid cells: cells can fall down, rise up, shift left, or shift right, stopping at the grid boundary or at non-background obstacle cells. Specific colors can be singled out to fall through background cells while being blocked by other colors. These operations model a wide class of visual transformation tasks where objects move under directed forces.

Introduction

Many visual reasoning tasks involve movement: an object falls to the floor, bubbles rise to the surface, debris shifts to one side. The gravity pack gives Mentova the ability to simulate and recognize these movements. A cell "falls" by joining all other non-background values in its column at the bottom, while background fills the vacated space at the top. A specific color can be made to "fall through" background cells while treating all other non-background cells as solid obstacles.

The pack is general-purpose. The four directions (down, up, left, right) cover the primary axes of movement on a grid. Color-specific settling and floating handle the common ARC-AGI pattern of one element type moving while others remain fixed.

Predicates

The gravity pack exports 14 predicates under the gv_* prefix.

Column utilities:
gv_col_values/3 extracts all values in a given column as a top-to-bottom list.
gv_set_col/4 replaces a column in a grid with a new list.

Direction operations:
gv_compact_col/3 and gv_fall_down/3 make all non-background cells sink to the bottom of their column.
gv_fall_up/3 makes non-background cells rise to the top.
gv_compact_row/3 and gv_fall_left/3 make non-background cells shift to the left of their row.
gv_fall_right/3 makes non-background cells shift to the right.

Color-specific settling:
gv_settle_color/4 makes cells of one specific color fall to the bottom of each column, while non-background non-color cells act as obstacles and stay above. Output column order: [Others] ++ [BGs] ++ [Colors].
gv_float_color/4 is the mirror: the color rises to the top. Output column order: [Colors] ++ [Others] ++ [BGs].
gv_stack_down/4 is an alias for gv_settle_color/4.
gv_stack_up/4 is an alias for gv_float_color/4.

Custom transforms:
gv_apply_col/4 applies a caller-supplied predicate to each column.
gv_apply_row/4 applies a caller-supplied predicate to each row.

Implementation

The core implementation uses two internal list primitives: gv_compact_list_bottom_/3 (non-BG to end) and gv_compact_list_top_/3 (non-BG to front). Both partition a list with include/3 and concatenate with append/2. Column-level operations are applied via foldl/4; row-level operations via maplist/3.

The color-specific predicates extend the partition into three categories - Color, Others, and BG - and concatenate in the order that achieves the desired settling behavior. The formula guarantees that the output list has the same length as the input list.

All 29 PLUnit acceptance tests pass. The pack depends only on library(lists) and library(apply).

Significance

The gravity pack closes a critical gap in Mentova's perception and transformation layer. Many ARC-AGI-2 tasks involve objects that fall, float, stack, or settle. With the gravity pack, Mentova can now:
- Detect that an output is just the input after falling (compare gv_fall_down output to actual output using cp_grids_equal)
- Apply settling as a post-processing step to candidate outputs
- Check whether a specific color settled under gravity

Combined with the induction pack (id_* predicates), the gravity pack enables a new class of hypothesis: "the output is the input after color X fell to the bottom."
