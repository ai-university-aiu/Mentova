The rule decodes a periodic projection pattern hidden in a left-side bar:
the bar's primary and secondary colors encode a period and a cell-position
formula that governs exactly how the right side of the grid is filled.

TASK DESCRIPTION

Task 1ae2feb7 presents grids split by a vertical divider column.
To the left of the divider, each row optionally carries a horizontal bar
of 1 or 2 non-background colors.

The right side of the divider is initially empty (all background).

The transformation fills the right side according to a rule derived entirely
from the bar.

RULE: BAR-EXTEND

Step 1 - find the divider column.

The divider is the unique column where every non-blank row carries the same
non-background value.

Step 2 - identify bar colors.

For each row with bar content, the rightmost non-background cell is the
primary color P with count CP (how many times P appears in the bar).
Any other non-background color is the secondary S with count CS.
If there is no secondary, CS defaults to 1.

Step 3 - compute period and pattern.

Period = CP times CS.

Within one period of positions 0 through Period-1:

• P appears at every position that is a multiple of CP.
• S appears at every position that is a multiple of CS and not already
  occupied by P.
• Background (0) fills all remaining positions.

Step 4 - fill right side.

The right side (all columns after the divider) is filled by repeating the
one-period pattern cyclically.

INSIGHT

The period = CP times CS formula creates a kind of least-common-multiple
interleaving: the primary color marks every CP-th slot and the secondary
marks every CS-th slot. When CP and CS are coprime, the pattern is maximally
interleaved. When one is a multiple of the other, one color dominates and
the other appears sparsely (or not at all). The bar itself is a compact
encoding of both the color palette and the rhythm of the fill.
