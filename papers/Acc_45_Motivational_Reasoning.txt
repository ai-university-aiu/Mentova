ABSTRACT

Rung 45 accomplishes motivational reasoning: identifying an agent's highest-
priority drive and the action it recommends. Demonstrated: alice top_drive
→ achieve_recognition (high); alice drive→action: achieve_recognition
→ compete_and_excel; Maslow hierarchy: safety needs precede esteem needs.
All answers include drive name, strength, and recommended action.


INTRODUCTION

Maslow's hierarchy of needs and goal-directed motivation theories hold
that agents prioritise drives according to urgency and salience. The most
pressing unsatisfied drive determines behaviour.

Pass criterion (Rung 45): "highest-priority drive identified for each agent."


METHOD

The motivational module stores: agent_drive(Agent, Drive, Strength, Action).
Implements: top_drive(Agent) → highest-strength drive; drive_to_action(Drive)
→ recommended action; hierarchy(Need) → Maslow-level preconditions.


THE DEMONSTRATION

    top_drive(alice): → drive(alice, achieve_recognition, high)
    top_drive(bob):   → drive(bob, belong_and_connect, high)

    drive_to_action(achieve_recognition): → compete_and_excel
    drive_to_action(ensure_safety):       → avoid_threat

    % Demonstrate the 'hierarchy' query (result shown on next line).
    hierarchy(esteem):
      → must_first_satisfy([physiological, safety, belonging])


RESULTS

Pass criterion met: the highest-strength drive is identified per agent and
the recommended action is returned alongside Maslow preconditions.


SIGNIFICANCE

Motivational reasoning enables Mentova to explain why agents act — not merely
what they do. Every action recommendation traces to a drive strength.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

Drive strengths are static. Dynamic drive satisfaction (eating resolves
hunger drive) is not modelled.
