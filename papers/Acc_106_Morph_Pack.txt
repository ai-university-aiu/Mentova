Accomplishment 106: Morphological Grid Operations - The Morph Pack

Author: D. R. Dison, Founder of AIU, Creator and Owner of PrologAI and Mentova, ORCID 0009-0001-9246-5758, linkedin.com/in/d-r-dison/

Date: 2026-06-25

Abstract

Mentova's cognitive substrate gains its 84th work package: the morph pack (mo_* predicate family, Layer 65). This pack provides 14 predicates implementing morphological operations on grid color channels: dilation, erosion, opening, closing, boundary extraction, ring extraction, hole filling, and grid padding/unpadding. Morphological operations transform the shape of a colored region rather than its content, making them the natural tool for tasks that require expanding, shrinking, outlining, or cleaning up visual objects.

Introduction

After the connect pack identifies objects in a grid, the next question is often: how should this object be transformed? Many visual reasoning tasks require shape transformations that go beyond color substitution:

- "Expand this object by one cell in every direction" (dilation)
- "Remove the single isolated pixels" (opening)
- "Fill the hole inside this closed shape" (close or fill_holes)
- "Draw an outline one cell outside this object" (ring)
- "Get just the border of this object" (boundary)

These are the operations of mathematical morphology, a classical discipline in image processing. The morph pack implements them symbolically in Prolog, operating on PrologAI's list-of-lists grid format.

Predicates

The morph pack exports 14 predicates under the mo_* prefix.

Dilation and erosion:
mo_dilate4/4 expands a Color region by one step in 4 cardinal directions.
mo_dilate8/4 expands using 8-connected adjacency (includes diagonals).
mo_erode4/4 shrinks a Color region: cells with any out-of-bounds or non-Color 4-neighbor become BG.
mo_erode8/4 shrinks using 8-connected erosion.
mo_dilate4_n/5 and mo_erode4_n/5 apply their respective operations N times.

Opening and closing:
mo_open4/4 is erode followed by dilate. It removes small protrusions and isolated pixels.
mo_close4/4 is dilate followed by erode. It fills small gaps within a region.

Boundary and ring:
mo_boundary4/3 returns Color cells that are not fully surrounded by Color in 4 directions (the inner contour).
mo_boundary8/3 does the same using 8-connectivity.
mo_ring4/4 returns BG cells that are 4-adjacent to at least one Color cell (the outer one-cell frame).

Structural:
mo_fill_holes4/4 fills BG cells enclosed inside Color: floods BG from all grid-border BG cells, then fills any BG cells not reached.
mo_pad/3 adds a one-cell border of a chosen color around the entire grid.
mo_unpad/2 removes the outermost ring from all four sides.

Implementation

All grid-level operations use a rebuild-from-scratch pattern: for each (R,C) in the output grid, a cell predicate computes the output color based on the current grid values. This is implemented via mo_rebuild_/5, which uses findall over row and column index lists.

The per-cell predicates (mo_dilate4_cell_, mo_erode4_cell_, etc.) use two helper predicates:
- mo_has_color4_/6: true if any in-bounds 4-neighbor has Color (uses cut for determinism)
- mo_all4_color_/6: true if all in-bounds 4-neighbors have Color; OOB neighbors count as not-Color and cause failure

forall/2, a SWI-Prolog built-in, is used in mo_all4_color_ and mo_all8_color_ to check all neighbors simultaneously.

mo_fill_holes4 uses an internal DFS flood fill (mo_flood_bg_) rather than depending on the connect pack, keeping the morph pack self-contained.

Significance

The morph pack gives Mentova the ability to transform the shapes of visual objects. Combined with the connect pack, Mentova can now: identify objects, extract their boundaries, expand or contract them, fill enclosed holes, and clean up noisy patterns. These operations cover a large fraction of the spatial transformation tasks in ARC-AGI-2.
