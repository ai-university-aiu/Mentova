Abstract

Mentova's cognitive substrate gains its 94th work package: the sym pack (sy_* predicate family, Layer 75). This pack provides 14 predicates for spatial symmetry transforms and symmetry testing. All 8 elements of the dihedral group D4 (the symmetry group of a square) are represented: the 6 distinct transforms plus 4 symmetry detection predicates covering horizontal, vertical, and rotational symmetry. The pack also provides symmetrization operations and full orbit computation.

Introduction

Symmetry is a fundamental structural property of visual reasoning. Many reasoning tasks contain grids that are symmetric: a pattern that looks the same when flipped horizontally, when rotated 180 degrees, or under a combination of transforms. The sym pack gives Mentova the ability to:

  Detect symmetry: does this grid have horizontal, vertical, or rotational symmetry?
  Apply transforms: what does this grid look like rotated 90 degrees clockwise?
  Symmetrize: given an asymmetric grid, what is the nearest horizontally symmetric grid?
  Compute orbit: what are all distinct spatial variants of this grid?

These operations are useful for any task involving spatial reasoning, not only for pattern recognition in visual puzzles.

Predicates

The sym pack exports 14 predicates under the sy_* prefix.

Spatial transforms:
sy_reflect_h/2     reflect left-right (each row reversed)
sy_reflect_v/2     reflect top-bottom (row order reversed)
sy_transpose/2     reflect across main diagonal (rows become columns)
sy_rotate90/2      rotate 90 degrees clockwise
sy_rotate180/2     rotate 180 degrees
sy_rotate270/2     rotate 270 degrees clockwise (= 90 counter-clockwise)

Symmetry tests:
sy_has_h_symm/1    grid equals its horizontal reflection
sy_has_v_symm/1    grid equals its vertical reflection
sy_has_rot2_symm/1 grid invariant under 180-degree rotation
sy_has_rot4_symm/1 grid invariant under 90-degree rotation

Symmetry discovery and enforcement:
sy_symmetries/2    list all symmetry names present (h, v, rot2, rot4)
sy_make_h_symm/2   make horizontally symmetric by mirroring left half
sy_make_v_symm/2   make vertically symmetric by mirroring top half
sy_d4_orbit/2      compute all distinct D4 group transforms

Implementation

All rotations are defined compositionally from the three basic operations:
  rotate90  = transpose then reflect_h
  rotate180 = reflect_v then reflect_h
  rotate270 = reflect_h then transpose

The anti-diagonal reflection (the 8th D4 element, used in sy_d4_orbit) is
computed as rotate90 then reflect_v.

Symmetry tests use structural unification: sy_has_h_symm(G) succeeds when
sy_reflect_h(G, G) succeeds, that is, when G unifies with its own reflection.

sy_make_h_symm and sy_make_v_symm use if-then-else to handle the center
element in odd-dimension cases without choicepoints.

sy_d4_orbit uses sort/2 to deduplicate the 8 candidate transforms.
For an all-same-color grid, all 8 transforms are identical and the orbit
contains only 1 element. For a generic asymmetric grid, up to 8 elements appear.

Significance

The sym pack completes the spatial perception layer. Combined with the grid,
transform, connect, morph, scene, and other perception packs, Mentova can now:

- Detect whether a grid exhibits structural symmetry
- Find all spatial variants of a pattern
- Symmetrize a partial observation
- Compare two grids modulo spatial transformation (by comparing their orbits)
