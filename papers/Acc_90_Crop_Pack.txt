Accomplishment 90: Crop Pack - Subgrid Extraction, Padding, Splitting, and Embedding

D. R. Dison, Founder of AIU, Creator of PrologAI and Mentova, ORCID 0009-0001-9246-5758, linkedin.com/in/d-r-dison/

Abstract

PrologAI now includes the crop pack (module crop, prefix cr_), a set of 14
predicates for rectangular subgrid operations: bounding box detection, content-
aware cropping, padding, border removal, horizontal and vertical splitting, row
and column band extraction, stitching, subgrid embedding, center extraction, and
quadrant splitting. The crop pack is the 68th work package in PrologAI and
Mentova's 90th accomplishment.

Introduction

Almost every visual reasoning task involves boundaries. Where does the content
start and end? Which region of the grid contains the relevant information? How
do we extract it cleanly so that downstream reasoning operates only on what
matters?

The crop pack is the answer: a complete set of predicates for finding, cutting,
and reconstructing rectangular regions of grids. Before this pack, isolating
the content of a grid or extracting a quadrant required inline list manipulation
scattered through every solver. Now cr_crop_content, cr_split_h, and
cr_quadrants are named operations that can be called in one step.

Design

The crop pack requires only the grid pack. All predicates work on lists-of-lists
of integer color values, the native grid representation throughout PrologAI. All
indices are 0-indexed and consistent with the conventions of the grid, scene,
path, and analogy packs.

All predicates are deterministic. Splitting predicates fail gracefully on
out-of-range inputs rather than throwing errors.

Bounding Box and Content Cropping

cr_bbox/3 finds the tight bounding box of all cells that differ from a given
background color. It returns bbox(MinR, MinC, MaxR, MaxC) and fails if every
cell equals the background.

cr_crop_bbox/6 extracts the rectangular subgrid at inclusive row/col bounds.
cr_crop_content/3 combines both: it finds the content bounding box and crops
to it in one step, stripping all background borders from all four sides.

Padding and Border Removal

cr_pad/4 adds a uniform N-cell border of a given color around the entire grid.
cr_strip_border/3 removes N rows and columns from every side. These two
predicates are exact inverses: cr_strip_border(cr_pad(G,C,N),N) = G.

Splitting

cr_split_h/4 divides a grid into top and bottom parts at row R. The top part
has R rows and the bottom part has the rest. cr_split_v/4 divides into left
and right parts at column C.

cr_rows/4 and cr_cols/4 provide the more general case: extracting any
contiguous band of rows or columns by 0-indexed From (inclusive) and To
(exclusive) bounds.

Joining and Embedding

cr_stitch_h/3 and cr_stitch_v/3 join two grids side by side or top to bottom.
They are the inverses of cr_split_h and cr_split_v, making it possible to
decompose a grid into parts, transform each part independently, and reassemble
the result.

cr_embed/5 places a subgrid at any row/column offset inside a base grid,
overwriting the cells at those positions. This is the fundamental operation for
constructing output grids by assembling parts.

Center and Quadrants

cr_center/4 extracts the centered Rows x Cols subgrid from a larger grid.
The offset is computed as (GridHeight - Rows) // 2 and (GridWidth - Cols) // 2.

cr_quadrants/5 splits a grid into four equal (or near-equal) quadrants at the
midpoint row and column. This is the primary decomposition operation for tasks
that exhibit four-fold structure.

Conclusion

The crop pack gives Mentova the ability to isolate, decompose, and reconstruct
any rectangular region of any grid. Combined with the grid, color, shape,
relation, path, symmetry, pattern, scene, sequence, and frame packs, Mentova
can now: find objects (scene, path), describe them (color, shape, relation),
understand their structure (symmetry, pattern, sequence), extract and isolate
them (crop), and reconstruct output grids by embedding transformed parts back
into a base (crop). The perceptual foundation is now complete.
