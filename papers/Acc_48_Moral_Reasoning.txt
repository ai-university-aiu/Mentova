ABSTRACT

We report Rung 48 — the final rung — of Mentova's 48-rung reasoning ladder: moral reasoning.
Mentova analyses moral dilemmas from three ethical frameworks (utilitarian, deontological,
virtue ethics) and synthesises an integrated verdict. For the trolley problem: utilitarian
verdict = pull_lever (save five), deontological verdict = pull_lever (no rule broken),
virtue verdict = do_nothing (virtuous restraint). Integrated: pull_lever (plurality).
Pass criterion met: dilemma analysed from multiple frameworks with integrated verdict.


INTRODUCTION

Moral reasoning is the apex of Mentova's reasoning ladder. It requires integrating
consequentialist, deontological, and virtue-based perspectives on ethical dilemmas.
Rung 48 implements all three frameworks and a plurality-vote integration.


BACKGROUND

Three major ethical traditions: utilitarianism (Bentham/Mill) — maximise welfare;
deontology (Kant) — follow universal rules; virtue ethics (Aristotle) — act as a
virtuous person would. Each gives different verdicts on hard cases.


METHOD

A new module, src/mentova/moral.pl, implements:

    mentova_moral(+Query, -Result, -Justification)

Four dilemmas: trolley_problem, lying_to_protect, doctors_dilemma, whistleblowing.
Three frameworks with independent verdict predicates. Integrated judgement by
plurality vote. Queries: analyse, utilitarian, deontological, virtue, describe, what_dilemmas.


DEMONSTRATION

    % Query Mentova for moral reasoning about 'analyse(trolley_problem)'.
    ?- mentova_query(moral, analyse(trolley_problem), R).
    R = answer(moral_analysis(trolley_problem,
                               utilitarian(pull_lever),
                               deontological(pull_lever),
                               virtue(do_nothing),
                               integrated(pull_lever)), ...)

    % Query Mentova for moral reasoning about 'virtue(doctors_dilemma)'.
    ?- mentova_query(moral, virtue(doctors_dilemma), R).
    R = answer(verdict(refuse), ...)


RESULTS

Trolley: utilitarian and deontological agree on pull_lever; virtue recommends do_nothing.
Integrated: pull_lever (2 of 3 frameworks). Doctors dilemma virtue verdict: refuse.
Pass criterion met.


SIGNIFICANCE

Moral reasoning is the most demanding capability for an AI system. Mentova does not
impose a single framework but shows all three verdicts transparently, leaving the
integrated judgement open to human review. This is the spirit of PrologAI: glass-box
reasoning where every conclusion is justified.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

Dilemma options and their moral properties are manually specified. Real moral
reasoning would require situational parsing, value learning, and cultural sensitivity.
