ABSTRACT

Rung 46 accomplishes informal reasoning: identifying logical fallacies by
name, type, and explanatory note. Demonstrated: arg1 → ad_hominem ('attacks
the person not the argument'); arg2 → straw_man ('misrepresents the
opponent's position'); arg3 → false_dilemma ('presents only two options
when more exist'); arg4 → slippery_slope ('assumes one step leads
inevitably to extreme outcome'). All detections include the fallacy name
and rationale.


INTRODUCTION

Informal logic catalogues rhetorical and argumentative errors that are
valid-seeming but fail on closer examination. Detecting them is essential
for critical thinking and adversarial reasoning.

Pass criterion (Rung 46): "fallacy named and explained for given argument."


METHOD

The informal module stores: informal_fallacy(ArgId, FallacyName, Type,
Explanation). Implements: detect_fallacy(ArgId) → fallacy name and
explanation; list_fallacies → all known fallacy types.


THE DEMONSTRATION

    detect_fallacy(arg1): → fallacy(arg1, ad_hominem, 'attacks the person not the argument')
    detect_fallacy(arg2): → fallacy(arg2, straw_man, 'misrepresents the opponent\'s position')
    detect_fallacy(arg3): → fallacy(arg3, false_dilemma, 'presents only two options when more exist')
    detect_fallacy(arg4): → fallacy(arg4, slippery_slope, 'assumes one step leads inevitably to extreme outcome')


RESULTS

Pass criterion met: each argument is matched to its named fallacy with
a one-sentence explanation drawn from the informal_fallacy/4 knowledge base.


SIGNIFICANCE

Informal reasoning gives Mentova the ability to critique arguments, not just
evaluate them logically. A valid-seeming inference that commits a fallacy
should be flagged, not accepted.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

Fallacy detection requires arguments to be pre-coded. Detecting fallacies
in natural-language text requires additional parsing not implemented here.


REPRODUCIBILITY

    swipl -l src/mentova/mentova.pl \
          -g "mentova_query(informal, detect_fallacy(arg1), R), write(R), nl" -t halt
