Abstract

PrologAI Work Package 243 adds the gridpos pack (gps_*, Layer 222): fourteen
predicates for extracting sub-grids and cell positions based on geometric
position within a raw grid. Operations cover halves, quadrants, even/odd rows
and columns, checkerboard patterns, center cell, corner cells, and the
horizontal/vertical cross through the center. All 44 acceptance tests pass.


1. Motivation

Grid-reasoning tasks frequently require selecting a spatially defined portion of
a grid: the top half, a particular quadrant, every other row, or the four corner
cells. These operations appear as pre-processing steps (extract a region),
post-processing steps (isolate the center cross after a transformation), and
hypothesis steps (is the pattern confined to the even columns?). The gridpos pack
provides all of these as first-class, tested predicates.


2. Architecture

Layer 222 sits immediately above gridhist (Layer 221) in the Data Layer.
Uses library(lists): nth0/3, member/2. No cross-pack dependencies.

Four private helpers support the public interface:
- gps_dims_/3: grid dimensions.
- gps_col_/3: extract a column as a top-to-bottom list.
- gps_subgrid_/6: extract any rectangular sub-grid by row/col bounds.
- gps_row_parity_/3: extract even or odd-indexed cells from a single row.


3. Predicates

Halves:

gps_top_half/2      - rows [0, H//2)
gps_bottom_half/2   - rows [H//2, H)
gps_left_half/2     - columns [0, W//2)
gps_right_half/2    - columns [W//2, W)

Quadrants:

gps_quadrant/3      - one of tl/tr/bl/br as a rectangular sub-grid

Row and column parity:

gps_even_rows/2     - rows at even 0-indexed positions (0, 2, 4, ...)
gps_odd_rows/2      - rows at odd 0-indexed positions (1, 3, 5, ...)
gps_even_cols/2     - columns at even 0-indexed positions
gps_odd_cols/2      - columns at odd 0-indexed positions

Checkerboard:

gps_checkerboard/3  - R-C pairs where (R+C) mod 2 = Parity (Parity bound to 0 or 1)

Center and corners:

gps_center_cell/3   - center coordinates (H//2, W//2)
gps_corners/2       - four R-C-Value terms in tl/tr/bl/br order

Center cross:

gps_cross_h/3       - center row index and row content
gps_cross_v/3       - center column index and column content as a list


4. Example Session

:- use_module(pack(gridpos/prolog/gridpos)).

G = [[a,b,c,d],[e,f,g,h],[i,j,k,l],[m,n,o,p]].

gps_top_half(G, T).          % T = [[a,b,c,d],[e,f,g,h]]
gps_quadrant(G, br, Q).      % Q = [[k,l],[o,p]]
gps_even_rows(G, E).         % E = [[a,b,c,d],[i,j,k,l]]
gps_checkerboard(G, 0, Cs).  % Cs = [0-0,0-2,1-1,1-3,2-0,2-2,3-1,3-3]
gps_center_cell(G, R, C).    % R = 2, C = 2
gps_corners(G, Cs).          % Cs = [0-0-a, 0-3-d, 3-0-m, 3-3-p]
gps_cross_v(G, C, Col).      % C = 2, Col = [c,g,k,o]


5. Test Coverage

packs/gridpos/test/test_gridpos.pl. Tests cover top/bottom halves (6),
left/right halves (6), quadrants (4), even/odd rows (6), even/odd cols (6),
checkerboard (4), center cell (3), corners (3), cross_h (3), cross_v (3).
Tests use three canonical grids: 4x4, 3x3, and 1x1.

7. Conclusion

The gridpos pack provides complete positional sub-grid extraction and cell
identification for raw grids. The fourteen gps_* predicates cover halves,
quadrants, parity-based row/column selection, checkerboard patterns, center cell,
corner cells, and center cross. This completes the geometric position family of
grid operations and enables downstream reasoning about where features appear
within a grid.
