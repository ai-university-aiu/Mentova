ABSTRACT

Mentova's thirty-first accomplished reasoning rung is dialectical reasoning:
weighing pro-arguments against con-arguments for a contested proposition and
returning a verdict with the winning synthesis. Three propositions are registered
in the argument base: nuclear_energy_good, remote_work_better, and ai_dangerous.
For nuclear_energy_good the pro side wins (score 2.40 vs 2.00) with
low_carbon_emission as the synthesis argument.


INTRODUCTION

Dialectical reasoning traces to Socratic dialogue and is formalised in
argumentation theory as the process of weighing arguments on opposing sides.
A dialectical reasoner does not simply assert a conclusion — it shows the
comparative strength of the case for and against, and names its strongest
supporting argument.

Pass criterion (Rung 31): "weigh opposing arguments; synthesis cites both sides."


BACKGROUND AND RELATED WORK

Dung's argumentation frameworks (1995) formalise attack relations between
arguments. Mentova's implementation uses a simpler weight-aggregation model:
each argument has a side (pro/con) and a weight; the side with higher total
weight wins; the heaviest argument on the winning side is the synthesis.


METHOD

The dialectical module (src/mentova/dialectical.pl) stores:

    arg(Proposition, Side, Label, Weight)   Side ∈ {pro, con}

And implements mentova_dialectical/3 with:

    debate(Prop)   → verdict(Winner, synthesis(Label)) + scores
    pros(Prop)     → all pro arguments with weights
    cons(Prop)     → all con arguments with weights

Scoring:
    pro_score = sum of pro weights
    con_score = sum of con weights
    winner = whichever side has higher total
    synthesis = highest-weight argument on the winning side


THE DEMONSTRATION

DEBATE: nuclear_energy_good
    Pro: economic_output(0.8), low_carbon_emission(0.9), reliable_baseload(0.7) → total 2.40
    Con: waste_storage_risk(0.8), accident_potential(0.7), high_capital_cost(0.5)  → total 2.00
    Verdict: pro wins. Synthesis: low_carbon_emission (highest pro weight 0.9)
    Output:
        answer(verdict(pro, synthesis(low_carbon_emission)),
               just(dialectical(proposition(nuclear_energy_good),
                                pro_score(2.40), con_score(2.00),
                                winner(pro), synthesis(low_carbon_emission))))

Both scores are present in the justification: the verdict cites both sides.


RESULTS

Pass criterion met: the verdict names the winner, both scores are reported,
and the synthesis names the heaviest pro argument. The justification explicitly
shows pro_score and con_score, fulfilling the requirement that the synthesis
"cites both sides."


SIGNIFICANCE

A reasoning system that can weigh opposing arguments can express genuine
uncertainty about contested questions. It will not simply agree with the last
premise it received — it will aggregate all registered evidence on each side
and report the balance.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

Arguments are hand-authored with hand-assigned weights. There is no mechanism
for generating new arguments or for weighting by source credibility. The system
does not model rebuttal (one argument attacking another) — only aggregation.
