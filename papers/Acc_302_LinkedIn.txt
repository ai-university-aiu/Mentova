Mentova just solved its fourth ARC-AGI-2 evaluation task using a new glass-box rule called segment_equalize.

The rule: non-background cells in the grid form linear segments in one direction (horizontal row, vertical column, or anti-diagonal r+c=const). One endpoint of every segment is already aligned to a common value. The other endpoint is wrong in some segments and must be extended or trimmed to match the middle segment's length.

What makes this interesting is the alignment concept for anti-diagonal segments. The invariant is not the raw row number of the endpoint but its r-c value (2*row - key). All segments that share an anchor will have the same r-c at that endpoint even though their row numbers differ. Once you know the anchor end and the target r-c, the correct row for each segment is (key + target_r_minus_c) // 2. Clean, exact, zero ambiguity.

The rule handles all three directions with the same algorithm. Direction is detected automatically from the input. No task-specific configuration.
