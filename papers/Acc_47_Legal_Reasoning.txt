ABSTRACT

We report Rung 47 of Mentova's 48-rung reasoning ladder: legal reasoning.
Mentova applies legal rules with exceptions and cites case precedents.
Scenario: "person takes property belonging to another without consent" → verdict:
liable(theft). Add "property taken to prevent greater harm" → exception applies →
exempt(necessity). Precedent: Donoghue v Stevenson establishes manufacturer
duty of care. Pass criterion met: rule applied, exception correctly overrides, precedent cited.


INTRODUCTION

Legal reasoning involves applying rules to facts, checking for exceptions, and
consulting precedents. Rung 47 implements a small statutory rule base, exception
catalogue, and case precedent registry.


BACKGROUND

Legal AI (Susskind, 1987) has studied rule-based legal reasoning since the 1980s.
The MYCIN-style rule-exception structure is directly applicable to legal syllogism.


METHOD

A new module, src/mentova/legal.pl, implements:

    mentova_legal(+Query, -Result, -Justification)

Five legal rules, five exceptions, four case precedents.
Queries: apply, precedent, exceptions_for, what_rules.


DEMONSTRATION

    % Query Mentova for legal reasoning about 'apply(r1'.
    ?- mentova_query(legal, apply(r1,
         ['person takes property belonging to another without consent']), R).
    R = answer(verdict(liable(theft)), ...)

    % Query Mentova for legal reasoning about 'apply(r1'.
    ?- mentova_query(legal, apply(r1,
         ['person takes property belonging to another without consent',
          'property taken to prevent greater harm']), R).
    R = answer(verdict(exempt(necessity)), ...)

    % Query Mentova for legal reasoning about 'precedent(case_donoghue_stevenson)'.
    ?- mentova_query(legal, precedent(case_donoghue_stevenson), R).
    R = answer(precedent(case_donoghue_stevenson, r4, liable(negligence),
                         'manufacturer owes duty of care to end consumer'), ...)


RESULTS

Theft rule applies → liable. Necessity exception overrides → exempt. Precedent cited.
Pass criterion met.


SIGNIFICANCE

Legal reasoning enables legal aid automation, contract analysis, compliance checking,
and judicial decision support.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

Rules and exceptions are simplified. Real legal reasoning requires statutory interpretation,
conflicting authority resolution, and natural language legal text parsing.
