ABSTRACT

We report Rung 36 of Mentova's 48-rung reasoning ladder: procedural reasoning.
Mentova retrieves step-by-step plans with preconditions and postconditions for
goals such as making tea, changing a tyre, or baking bread. For "make_tea,"
the plan has six steps. Step 3 is add_teabag, with preconditions [has(teabag),
cup_ready] and postconditions [teabag_in_cup]. Pass criterion met: complete plan
returned with preconditions and postconditions per step.


INTRODUCTION

Procedural reasoning concerns how goals are accomplished: what sequence of actions,
in what order, with what prerequisites. Rung 36 equips Mentova with four procedures
(make_tea, change_tyre, plant_seed, bake_bread) and precondition/postcondition tracking.


BACKGROUND

Procedural reasoning is central to planning (Fikes and Nilsson, 1971) and
robotics. STRIPS (Stanford Research Institute Problem Solver) introduced the
precondition/postcondition model used here.


METHOD

A new module, src/mentova/procedural.pl, implements:

    mentova_procedural(+Query, -Result, -Justification)

Queries: plan_for(Goal), execute(Goal, InitState), step_n(Goal, N), what_goals.


DEMONSTRATION

    % Query Mentova for procedural reasoning about 'step_n(make_tea'.
    ?- mentova_query(procedural, step_n(make_tea, 3), R).
    R = answer(step(3, add_teabag, [has(teabag), cup_ready], [teabag_in_cup]),
               just(procedural(step_lookup(make_tea, 3),
                               action(add_teabag),
                               preconditions([has(teabag), cup_ready]),
                               postconditions([teabag_in_cup]))))


RESULTS

Six-step plan for make_tea retrieved. Step 3: add_teabag. Preconditions and
postconditions named. Pass criterion met.


SIGNIFICANCE

Procedural reasoning enables task instruction, robotic planning, and
conversational AI that can explain how to accomplish goals step by step.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

Procedures are stored as static templates. A full planner would generate plans
automatically from action axioms and goal specifications.


REPRODUCIBILITY

Requirements: SWI-Prolog 9.0.4 or later.

  git clone https://github.com/ai-university-aiu/Mentova.git
  cd Mentova
  % Load the Mentova bootstrap into SWI-Prolog, execute the query, and halt.
  swipl -l src/mentova/mentova.pl \
        % Supply a goal or termination instruction to the SWI-Prolog command.
        -g "mentova_boot, mentova_query(procedural, plan_for(make_tea), R), write(R), nl" \
        % Supply a goal or termination instruction to the SWI-Prolog command.
        -t halt
