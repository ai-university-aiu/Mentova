ABSTRACT

Wave 44 adds the jigsaw_fill rule (WP-302, Layer 277) to PrologAI's ARC-AGI-2
benchmark solver, solving task 5dbc8537 and raising the score from 41/120 =
34.17% to 42/120 = 35.00%.

The rule recognizes that the input contains two regions separated by a split
point: a 2-color template (solid color S, hole color H) and a multi-color
objects region (background B plus shaped pieces). Each non-B 4-connected
component in the objects region is a "piece" whose normalized cell-offset shape
(possibly multi-colored) exactly matches one contiguous rectangular sub-region of
H-cells in the template. A backtracking exact cover assigns pieces to
non-overlapping placements; the filled template is the output.


TASK DESCRIPTION

Task ID: 5dbc8537

Input structure: a grid that splits at a row or column boundary into two parts.

Part 1 - Template: exactly 2 distinct colors (solid S and hole H). The four
corner cells all hold S, identifying S unambiguously. The H-cells form multiple
isolated rectangular regions embedded in the S-structure.

Part 2 - Objects region: background color B (most common) plus multiple isolated
multi-color shapes. Each shape is a 4-connected component of non-B cells whose
bounding box is exactly rectangular and fully filled.

Correspondence: for each piece, its normalized shape (set of (dr, dc, color)
triples relative to the bounding box top-left) matches exactly one available
rectangular sub-region of H-cells in the template. The pieces together tile all
H-cells with no overlaps and no gaps.

Output: the template with each H-cell replaced by the color of the piece assigned
to it. Multi-colored pieces preserve each cell's original color.


PATTERN ANALYSIS

Step 1 - Split detection: scan all possible row-major and column-major split
points. The unique split where the candidate template has exactly 2 colors AND
the total non-background cells in the objects region equals the total hole cells
in the template identifies the correct boundary.

Step 2 - Solid identification: the corner cell (top-left) always holds the solid
color S. The other color is H. This heuristic succeeds even when S and H appear
with equal frequency in the template.

Step 3 - Piece extraction: BFS from each unvisited non-background cell in the
objects region collects each 4-connected component together with the per-cell
colors (since components can be multi-colored, all non-background cells in the
component are harvested regardless of their individual color).

Step 4 - Placement search: for each normalized piece, all positions (R0, C0) are
enumerated such that every cell of the piece placed at that offset lands on an
H-cell in the template. Pieces are sorted by ascending number of valid placements
to guide backtracking toward the most constrained choices first.

Step 5 - Backtracking exact cover: process pieces in fewest-placements order.
For each piece, try each valid placement; verify all cells are still available in
the remaining hole set; mark them used; recurse. Backtrack on failure. The unique
tiling is found on the first successful path (no backtracking required in
practice for the given tasks, since each piece typically has at most one or two
valid placements after earlier pieces are placed).

Step 6 - Output: apply the resulting color map to the template, replacing each
H-cell with its assigned piece color and preserving all S-cells unchanged.


RESULTS

Both training pairs and the test case produce the correct output.

Training Pair 1 (15x15 input, 15x7 output): template = left 7 columns (solid 9,
hole 4); objects = right 8 columns (background 4); 7 pieces totaling 41 cells;
41 hole cells; unique tiling found via backtracking.

Training Pair 2 (20x20 input, 9x20 output): template = bottom 9 rows (solid 2,
hole 8); objects = top 11 rows (background 8); 9 pieces totaling 74 cells
including multi-colored pieces (4/7 checkerboard, 1/9 cross pattern); 74 hole
cells; unique tiling found.

Test Case (25x25 input, 10x25 output): template = top 10 rows (solid 3, hole 1);
objects = bottom 15 rows (background 1); 14 pieces totaling 125 cells; 125 hole
cells; unique tiling found via backtracking with fewest-placements-first ordering.
