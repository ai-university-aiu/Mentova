ABSTRACT

We report Rung 24 of Mentova's 48-rung reasoning ladder: case-based reasoning.
Mentova solves a new problem by retrieving the most similar past case and adapting
its solution. Given a flat tyre problem with tools available, Mentova retrieves
case c1 (similarity score 3/3) and returns the solution change_tyre. The retrieved
case yields the correct adapted solution. Pass criterion met.


INTRODUCTION

Case-based reasoning (CBR) is a problem-solving strategy that reuses solutions
from similar past experiences rather than deriving solutions from scratch. Rung 24
implements the Retrieve-Reuse cycle of CBR: find the most similar past case by
feature matching and return its solution.


BACKGROUND

CBR was formalised by Schank (1982) and Kolodner (1993). The core algorithm is
k-NN over feature spaces, similar to transductive reasoning (Rung 10) but focused
on problem-solution pairs rather than classification.


METHOD

A new module, src/mentova/case_based.pl, implements:

    mentova_cbr(+Query, -Result, -Justification)

Case library: 8 cases covering vehicle problems and survival scenarios.
Similarity: Hamming matching over feature=value pairs.

Query forms: solve(ProbFeatures), retrieve(ProbFeatures).


DEMONSTRATION

    ?- mentova_query(case_based, solve([problem=flat,vehicle=car,tools=yes]), R).
    R = answer(change_tyre, just(cbr(problem([...]), retrieved(c1,3), solution(change_tyre), cycle(retrieve_reuse))))

    Case c1 matched with score 3 (all 3 features match). Solution: change_tyre.


RESULTS

Correct solution retrieved. Similarity score 3/3.
Pass criterion met: retrieved case yields correct adapted solution.


SIGNIFICANCE

CBR gives Mentova experience-based reasoning. New problems are solved by analogy
to past experience without requiring rules to be formulated explicitly.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

Only the Retrieve and Reuse phases are implemented. The Revise and Retain phases
(modifying the solution and storing the new case) require interactive feedback.


REPRODUCIBILITY
