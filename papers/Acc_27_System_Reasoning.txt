ABSTRACT

We report Rung 27 of Mentova's 48-rung reasoning ladder: system reasoning.
Mentova reasons about how parts interact to produce whole-system behavior. For a
bicycle system, Mentova identifies six parts with roles (wheel/rolls, pedal/drives,
chain/transmits, etc.), traces the interaction chain pedal→chain→spins_wheel, and
derives the whole behavior "moves_forward" from the mechanism [wheel(rolls),
pedal(drives), chain(transmits)]. Pass criterion met: whole-behavior question
answered from parts.


INTRODUCTION

System reasoning requires understanding how individual components interact to
produce emergent whole-system behaviors. This is distinct from commonsense
reasoning about individual objects — it is about the collective behavior of
interacting parts.

Rung 27 equips Mentova with a system model containing parts, roles, interactions,
and emergent whole behaviors for three systems: bicycle, plant, and team.


BACKGROUND

Systems thinking (von Bertalanffy, 1968) studies how the behavior of complex systems
emerges from part interactions. In AI, this connects to qualitative process theory
(Forbus, 1984) and model-based reasoning (Rung 28).


METHOD

A new module, src/mentova/system_reasoning.pl, implements:

    mentova_system(+Query, -Result, -Justification)

Systems: bicycle (6 parts), plant (4 parts), team (3 parts).
Supported queries: parts, role, behavior, if_fails, trace.


DEMONSTRATION

    % Query Mentova for system reasoning about 'behavior(bicycle)'.
    ?- mentova_query(system, behavior(bicycle), R).
    R = answer(moves_forward, just(system_behavior(bicycle, moves_forward,
               mechanism([wheel(rolls), pedal(drives), chain(transmits)]))))

    Bicycle moves_forward via the mechanism of wheel, pedal, and chain.

    % Query Mentova for system reasoning about 'trace(bicycle'.
    ?- mentova_query(system, trace(bicycle, pedal, chain), R).
    R = answer(drives_chain, just(interaction_trace(bicycle,pedal,chain,effect(drives_chain))))


RESULTS

Whole behavior correctly derived from mechanism parts.
Pass criterion met: whole-behavior question answered from parts.


SIGNIFICANCE

System reasoning enables Mentova to answer "why does the system do X?" by naming
the parts and interactions responsible. It also supports failure analysis.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

Whole behaviors are stored explicitly rather than derived emergently from part
interactions. A full system simulator would compute emergent behaviors from dynamics.

        % Supply a goal or termination instruction to the SWI-Prolog command.
