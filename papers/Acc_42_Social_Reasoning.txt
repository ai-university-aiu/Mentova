ABSTRACT

We report Rung 42 of Mentova's 48-rung reasoning ladder: social reasoning.
Mentova reasons about social roles, relationships, trust levels, group memberships,
and applicable norms. In school context, alice is a teacher and her obligations are
prepare_lessons and treat_fairly. Bob trusts alice at level=high. Pass criterion met:
role, relationship, trust, and norms returned in context.


INTRODUCTION

Social reasoning requires understanding the structure of human relationships,
roles, and norms. It is foundational to social AI, dialogue systems, and
human-robot interaction.


BACKGROUND

Role theory (Biddle, 1986) studies how social positions carry expectations.
Multi-agent systems use social norms to coordinate agent behavior.


METHOD

A new module, src/mentova/social.pl, implements:

    mentova_social(+Query, -Result, -Justification)

Three agents (alice, bob, carol, mentor) in three contexts (school, workplace, family).
Relations, trust levels, group memberships, and norms fully encoded.


DEMONSTRATION

    % Query Mentova for social reasoning about 'role(alice'.
    ?- mentova_query(social, role(alice, school), R).
    R = answer(role(alice, teacher, school), ...)

    % Query Mentova for social reasoning about 'trust(bob'.
    ?- mentova_query(social, trust(bob, alice), R).
    R = answer(trust(bob, alice, high), ...)

    % Query Mentova for social reasoning about 'norms_for(teacher'.
    ?- mentova_query(social, norms_for(teacher, school), R).
    R = answer(norms(teacher, school, [prepare_lessons, treat_fairly]), ...)


RESULTS

Alice's role: teacher. Bob trusts alice: high. Teacher norms: prepare_lessons, treat_fairly.
Pass criterion met.


SIGNIFICANCE

Social reasoning is essential for AI systems that interact with people and operate
within organisations. Understanding roles and norms prevents inappropriate behavior.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

Social structures are manually encoded. Dynamic social inference from behaviour
would require learning from observation.


REPRODUCIBILITY

Requirements: SWI-Prolog 9.0.4 or later.

  git clone https://github.com/ai-university-aiu/Mentova.git
  cd Mentova
  % Load the Mentova bootstrap into SWI-Prolog, execute the query, and halt.
  swipl -l src/mentova/mentova.pl \
        % Supply a goal or termination instruction to the SWI-Prolog command.
        -g "mentova_boot, mentova_query(social, role(alice, school), R), write(R), nl" \
        % Supply a goal or termination instruction to the SWI-Prolog command.
        -t halt
