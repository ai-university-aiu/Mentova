Abstract

PrologAI gains its 210th work package: the scenerank pack, a suite of 14 rk_*
predicates that rank candidate symbolic rule terms by coverage and consistency
across a list of Before-After scene pair training examples.


Motivation

The ruleinfer pack can infer specific rule candidates from individual examples.
The scenepair pack characterizes what kind of transformation occurred. But in
a real reasoning task, multiple candidate rules may be plausible. Which one
actually explains all the training examples? The scenerank pack answers this
question by computing coverage: how many pairs does each candidate rule
correctly explain?


Coverage

Coverage is computed by applying the rule to each Before scene (using the same
dispatch vocabulary as sceneapply) and comparing the result to the After scene
using msort for order-insensitive equality. A pair is "covered" if the rule
transforms it correctly.

The pack includes: rk_coverage for the count, rk_coverage_pairs for the list
of covered pairs, rk_rule_score as an alias, rk_coverage_ratio for a Num/Den
fraction, and rk_n_pairs for the total pair count.


Consistency and Filtering

rk_consistent checks that a rule covers every pair. rk_inconsistent_pairs
finds the failures. rk_all_consistent filters a candidate list to those that
are fully consistent. rk_filter_min_coverage keeps only rules meeting a minimum
coverage threshold. rk_perfect_rules keeps rules whose coverage equals the
total pair count.


Ranking and Selection

rk_rank_rules sorts candidates by coverage descending (negated-count msort),
breaking ties by standard term order. rk_rank_candidates returns Coverage-Rule
pairs for display. rk_best_rule takes the head of the ranked list.
rk_top_n_rules returns the top N candidates as Coverage-Rule pairs.


Self-Contained Rule Engine

The pack implements its own rk_apply_ predicate with the complete sceneapply
rule vocabulary: recolor(Old,New), recolor_all(New), color_map(Map), shift(DR,DC),
to_origin, reflect_h(Width), reflect_v(Height), remove_color(Color),
keep_color(Color), sort_size_desc, sort_size_asc, top_n(N). This makes scenerank
independent of sceneapply, avoiding a cross-pack dependency.


Test Coverage

41 of 41 unit tests pass. Tests cover all 14 predicates, single and multi-pair
scenarios, correct and incorrect rules, tie-breaking in ranking, vacuous
consistency on empty pair lists, and rules beyond the basic recolor vocabulary
(remove_color, recolor_all).


Significance

The scenerank pack is the selection step in the symbolic learning loop. With
ruleinfer inferring candidates, scenepair characterizing the transformation,
and scenerank selecting the best rule, PrologAI now has a complete
infer-characterize-rank-apply pipeline for scene-level symbolic reasoning.
