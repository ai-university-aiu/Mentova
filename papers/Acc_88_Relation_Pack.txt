Accomplishment 88: Relation Pack - Spatial Relations Between Cell Regions

D. R. Dison, Founder of AIU, Creator of PrologAI and Mentova, ORCID 0009-0001-9246-5758, linkedin.com/in/d-r-dison/

Abstract

PrologAI now includes the relation pack (module relation, prefix rl_), a set
of 14 predicates for spatial relationships between cell regions. A region is
any list of r(R,C) cells. The pack provides positional ordering (above, below,
left, right), 4-connected adjacency, minimum Manhattan distance, bounding box
containment, set relations (overlap, disjoint), row and column alignment,
integer centroid computation, centroid offset, and cardinal direction. The
relation pack is the 66th work package in PrologAI and Mentova's 88th
accomplishment.

Introduction

Having a vocabulary for the properties of individual regions is not enough.
Visual reasoning requires understanding the spatial relationships between
regions - and between objects in the world in general.

Is object A above object B? Are they touching? How far apart are they? In
which direction would you travel to get from one to the other? These are the
questions that humans answer effortlessly when looking at an image, and they
are exactly the questions that ARC-AGI-2 tasks often require to be answered
before a transformation rule can be formulated.

The relation pack provides these as first-class predicates, each composable
with the other packs in the cognitive stack.

Design

The fundamental input to every relation predicate is a region: a list of
r(R,C) cells. This is intentionally the same representation as a shape
(before normalization) and the same representation as the cell sets returned
by pf_flood_fill and gd_objects. The relation pack connects seamlessly to
every other pack that works with cell lists.

All predicates are deterministic (they succeed or fail; they do not produce
multiple solutions) because spatial relations are truth values, not
generators.

Positional Ordering

rl_above/2 tests whether every row of A is strictly less than every row of B.
rl_below/2 is the converse. rl_left_of/2 and rl_right_of/2 test the same
condition on columns. These predicates are strict: if any cell of A shares a
row (or column) with B, rl_above fails.

Adjacency and Distance

rl_adjacent/2 tests whether any cell of A has a 4-connected neighbor in B.
This is the standard ARC adjacency: orthogonal, not diagonal.

rl_distance/3 returns the minimum Manhattan distance between any pair of
cells from A and B. When the regions overlap, the distance is 0.

Containment and Set Relations

rl_contained_bbox/2 tests whether the bounding box of A is entirely within
the bounding box of B. This is weaker than cell-level containment but faster
and sufficient for many reasoning tasks.

rl_overlap/2 and rl_disjoint/2 test for shared and non-shared cells respectively.

Row and Column Alignment

rl_same_row/2 and rl_same_col/2 test whether two regions share at least one
row or column index. These predicates capture grid-level alignment that is
independent of whether the regions actually share cells.

Centroid, Offset, and Direction

rl_centroid/3 computes the integer (floor division) average row and column of
a region. rl_offset/4 computes the centroid-to-centroid offset between two
regions. rl_direction/3 converts that offset to a cardinal direction (above,
below, left, or right) by finding the dominant axis.

Conclusion

The relation pack gives Mentova a complete first-order vocabulary for spatial
relationships. Combined with the path pack (connectivity), shape pack
(pattern identity), color pack (palette), and frame pack (borders), Mentova
can now describe the spatial layout of any grid at the level of: what objects
are present, what color and shape they have, how they are connected, what
border they form, and how they relate to each other in space. This vocabulary
is the foundation for composing transformation rules.
