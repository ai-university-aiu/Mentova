ABSTRACT

Rung 44 accomplishes emotional reasoning: primary appraisal (desirable or
threatening?), secondary appraisal (handle or flee?), and emotion prediction.
Demonstrated: alice/win_competition → joy/high/approach; bob/exam_tomorrow
→ anxiety/moderate/prepare; alice/lose_contest → sadness/moderate/withdraw.
All responses include emotion category, intensity, and action tendency.


INTRODUCTION

Lazarus's cognitive appraisal theory holds that emotions arise from a
two-stage appraisal: Is this event relevant and positive or negative?
(primary), and Can I cope? (secondary).

Pass criterion (Rung 44): "emotion correctly assigned from situation description."


METHOD

The emotional module stores: situation_appraisal(Agent, Event, Valence,
Coping, Emotion, Intensity, Tendency). Implements: appraise(Agent, Event)
→ emotion; how_might_feel(Agent, Situation) → predicted emotion with
appraisal rationale.


THE DEMONSTRATION

    % Demonstrate the 'appraise' query (result shown on next line).
    appraise(alice, win_competition):
      Valence: positive, Coping: approach
      → emotion(joy, high, approach)

    % Demonstrate the 'how might feel' query (result shown on next line).
    how_might_feel(bob, exam_tomorrow):
      Valence: negative, Coping: manageable
      → likely_emotion(anxiety, moderate, prepare)

    % Demonstrate the 'appraise' query (result shown on next line).
    appraise(alice, lose_contest):
      Valence: negative, Coping: unavoidable
      → emotion(sadness, moderate, withdraw)


RESULTS

Pass criterion met: emotion is correctly assigned for each appraisal pair.
Intensity reflects goal-relevance and coping capacity.


SIGNIFICANCE

Emotional reasoning allows Mentova to model affect — not to simulate feelings,
but to reason correctly about how emotional states influence behaviour and
decision-making for any agent in the knowledge base.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

Emotions are statically stored as appraisal triples. Emotion dynamics
(escalation, regulation, recovery) are not modelled.
