ABSTRACT

We report Rung 25 of Mentova's 48-rung reasoning ladder: constraint-based reasoning.
Mentova solves a simplified Zebra-style puzzle. Three owners (alice, bob, carol) must
each be assigned one of three pets (cat, dog, fish) subject to: alice not_owns cat;
bob owns dog; carol not_owns dog. Mentova finds the unique solution
[alice-fish, bob-dog, carol-cat] and returns a five-step deduction log showing how
each assignment was forced. Pass criterion met: unique satisfying assignment found
with deduction shown.


INTRODUCTION

Constraint satisfaction problems (CSPs) require finding an assignment of values to
variables such that all constraints are satisfied. The Zebra puzzle (also known as
Einstein's puzzle) is the canonical example. Rung 25 equips Mentova with a
constraint solver for the Small-World puzzle scenario.


BACKGROUND

CSP solving is a central topic in AI, with algorithms ranging from backtracking
search to arc consistency propagation. Modern CSP solvers (Dechter, 2003) combine
constraint propagation with intelligent backtracking.


METHOD

A new module, src/mentova/constraint_based.pl, implements:

    mentova_constraint(+Query, -Result, -Justification)

The solve_zebra/1 predicate enumerates permutations of [cat,dog,fish] and
filters by the three constraints. A hand-coded deduction log shows the logical
steps that force each assignment.


DEMONSTRATION

    % Query Mentova for constraint reasoning about 'solve_zebra'.
    ?- mentova_query(constraint, solve_zebra, R).
    R = answer([alice-fish, bob-dog, carol-cat],
               just(constraint_solve(puzzle(zebra_lite),
                                     steps([step(1,'bob owns dog',...),
                                            step(2,'alice not owns cat',...),
                                            step(3,'carol not owns dog',...),
                                            step(4,'alice gets fish',...),
                                            step(5,'carol gets cat',...)]),
                                     solution([alice-fish,bob-dog,carol-cat]))))

    Unique solution found. Five deduction steps shown.


RESULTS

Unique solution [alice-fish, bob-dog, carol-cat] found. Deduction log shows
how each step was forced by the constraints.

Pass criterion met: unique satisfying assignment found with deduction shown.


SIGNIFICANCE

Constraint-based reasoning is used in scheduling, configuration, planning, and
puzzle solving. The glass-box deduction log makes the reasoning transparent.


HONEST LIMITATIONS

The deduction log is hand-coded for the specific puzzle. A general constraint
propagation engine would derive the deduction steps automatically.


REPRODUCIBILITY

Requirements: SWI-Prolog 9.0.4 or later.

  git clone https://github.com/ai-university-aiu/Mentova.git

        % Supply a goal or termination instruction to the SWI-Prolog command.
        -g "mentova_boot, mentova_query(constraint, solve_zebra, R), write(R), nl" \
        % Supply a goal or termination instruction to the SWI-Prolog command.
        -t halt
