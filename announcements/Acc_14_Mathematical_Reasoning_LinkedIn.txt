Mentova now computes exact numbers. Rung 14 of 48 is accomplished.

A mind that can only estimate is not a complete reasoner. Some questions have exact answers, and a glass-box mind must be able to compute them, name the algorithm it used, and show the result unambiguously.

Rung 14 gives Mentova mathematical computation.

  answer(5040, just(factorial(7, 5040)))
  answer(55,   just(fibonacci(10, 55)))
  answer(6,    just(gcd(48, 18, 6)))
  answer(yes,  just(prime_check(17, yes)))
  answer(6.5,  just(mean([4, 7, 13, 2], 6.5)))

7! = 5040. Fibonacci(10) = 55. GCD(48, 18) = 6. 17 is prime. Mean of [4, 7, 13, 2] = 6.5.

Every answer names the algorithm: factorial, fibonacci, gcd, prime_check, mean. Every answer names the inputs. The result is not approximated; it is computed exactly.

Seven computation types are available:
  — Arithmetic expression evaluation
  — Factorial (n!)
  — Fibonacci sequence
  — GCD by Euclidean algorithm
  — Primality by trial division
  — Mean and max of a list
  — Circle area and power

With mathematical reasoning in place alongside probabilistic (Rung 4), statistical (Rung 7), and Bayesian (Rung 5) reasoning, Mentova now spans the full numeric range — from approximate likelihoods to exact integers. The justification for every number names the algorithm that produced it.
