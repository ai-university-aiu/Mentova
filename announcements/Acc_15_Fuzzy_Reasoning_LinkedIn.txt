Mentova now reasons in degrees. Rung 15 of 48 is accomplished.

Is 22°C warm? Binary logic says yes or no. Fuzzy logic says: warm with degree 0.7.

That 0.7 is meaningful. It tells you 22°C is definitively in the warm range but not at its centre (25°C). It tells you some coolness remains. It allows the mind to say: high confidence of warmth, moderate residual coolness.

Rung 15 gives Mentova fuzzy membership functions and linguistic modifiers.

Ask Mentova to classify 22°C:

  answer(best(warm, 0.7, high),
         just(fuzzy_classify(temperature, 22, best(warm, 0.7), modifier(high))))

22°C is warm. Degree: 0.7. Linguistic modifier: high. Not "very high" (0.8+), not "somewhat" (0.4-0.6). High.

Ask for the explicit warm membership:

  answer(degree(warm, 0.7), just(fuzzy_membership(temperature, 22, warm, 0.7)))

The membership function for warm is triangular, centred at 25°C, falling to zero at 15°C and above 35°C. At 22°C, you are 0.7 of the way toward peak warmth.

Ask about speed — 75 km/h:

  answer(best(medium, 0.875, very_high),
         just(fuzzy_classify(speed, 75, best(medium, 0.875), modifier(very_high))))

75 km/h is medium-speed with degree 0.875 — very high membership in the medium category. Not fast (would need >100 km/h), not slow (<50 km/h). Solidly medium.

The five linguistic modifiers: very_high (≥0.8), high (0.6-0.8), somewhat (0.4-0.6), low (0.2-0.4), very_low (<0.2).

Fuzzy operations: AND is min, OR is max, NOT is 1 minus degree. All named in justifications.

Why does graded truth matter? Because the world is graded. "The patient has a high fever" is not a Boolean. "The car is moving fast" is not a Boolean. A mind that can only say yes or no is forced to draw arbitrary cutoffs. A mind that can say 0.7 and high is closer to the way language actually works.

Fuzzy reasoning is the foundation for qualitative reasoning (Rung 16), which predicts directions of change, and for heuristic reasoning (Rung 29), which works within satisfaction bounds.
