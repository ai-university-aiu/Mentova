PrologAI gains the pivot pack - 14 predicates for applying D4 symmetry operations
to cell lists centered at any chosen pivot position.

The D4 group is the full set of symmetries of a square: identity, three rotations
(90 CW, 180, 90 CCW), and four reflections (horizontal, vertical, diagonal,
anti-diagonal).

The pivot pack applies all 8 of these operations to lists of grid cells, with any
cell as the rotation/reflection center.

What the pivot pack provides:

• pv_centroid - integer floor centroid of a cell list
• pv_to_rel / pv_from_rel - convert between absolute and pivot-relative coordinates
• pv_rotate_cells_cw / 180 / ccw - rotate a cell list 90 CW, 180, or 90 CCW
• pv_reflect_cells_h / v / diag / antidiag - reflect across 4 symmetry axes
• pv_orbit - all distinct cells reachable by D4 operations from one starting cell
• pv_sym_closure - smallest D4-symmetric set containing a given cell list
• pv_stamp_at - paint values at offset positions in a grid (out-of-bounds skipped)

Why it matters:

The sym pack applies isometries to a full grid matrix.

The step pack traces paths and rays from a cell in a direction.

The pivot pack fills the space between them: it rotates or reflects a localized
object - a list of cell positions - around an arbitrary anchor point, without
touching the rest of the grid.

This enables per-object symmetry testing, shape enumeration across all D4
equivalents, and stamping rotated object copies back into a grid.
