Mentova Accomplishment 176: The Integer Median Computation Pack

PrologAI gains its 156th work package today.

The median pack (Layer 135, md_* prefix) provides 14 predicates for computing
the lower (floor) integer median of lists, grid rows, columns, and the entire
grid, plus 4-connected and 8-connected median filters, and above/below-median
cell selection.

The median is the middle value of a sorted sequence.

It is more resistant to outliers than the mean: a single extreme value cannot
pull the median away from the center of the data.

Why msort and not sort? sort/2 removes duplicates. For [1, 1, 1, 2, 3],
sort gives [1, 2, 3] and the "median" would be 2. But the correct median
of [1, 1, 1, 2, 3] is 1. The median pack uses msort/2 throughout to preserve
duplicates and give the correct answer.

The 14 predicates cover:

Single-list median: md_median/2.

Per-row and per-column medians: md_row/3, md_col/3, md_row_medians/2,
md_col_medians/2.

Grid-wide median: md_grid/2.

Median filters: md_filter4/2 and md_filter8/2 replace each cell with the
median of itself and its in-bounds 4 or 8 neighbors - smoothing grids while
preserving edges better than a mean filter.

Above/below selection: md_above/2, md_below/2, md_row_above/3, md_row_below/3,
md_col_above/3, md_col_below/3 - finding which cells are above or below the
applicable median.
