Mentova now checks its own proofs against its kernel. Rung 13 of 48 is accomplished.

Every reasoning system makes claims. But can it verify that its claims follow only from sanctioned rules? Can it examine a proof and tell you whether every step is legitimate?

Rung 13 gives Mentova a proof checker against the Minimal PrologAI Kernel (MPK).

The MPK defines four and only four allowed proof transitions:

  MPK-1: Fact — A is in the knowledge base, therefore A is provable.
  MPK-2: Modus Ponens — if A→B is in KB and A is proved, then B is proved.
  MPK-3: Conjunction — if A is proved and B is proved, then A∧B is proved.
  MPK-4: IsA Chain — if is_a(X,Y) and is_a(Y,Z) hold, then is_a(X,Z) holds.

Submit a proof. Mentova checks every step.

Derivation: prove is_a(canary, animal) from a KB containing is_a(canary,bird) and is_a(bird,animal).

Steps:
  1. step(fact, is_a(canary,bird), [])
  2. step(fact, is_a(bird,animal), [])
  3. step(isa_chain, is_a(canary,animal), [is_a(canary,bird), is_a(bird,animal)])

Result:

  answer(valid, just(mpk_check(..., result(valid), report([
    check(step(fact, is_a(canary,bird), []), ok),
    check(step(fact, is_a(bird,animal), []), ok),
    check(step(isa_chain, is_a(canary,animal), [...]), ok)
  ]))))

All three steps: ok. Proof: valid.

Step 1 uses MPK-1. Step 2 uses MPK-1. Step 3 uses MPK-4 — IsA transitivity. Every step is sanctioned by the kernel.

Now try submitting a step that invents a transition the kernel does not recognise. Mentova marks it invalid and names the offending step. The kernel holds.

This is what formal reasoning means in a cognitive architecture: not just deriving conclusions, but being able to verify that the derivation respects the foundational specification. It is the difference between a system that says "trust me" and one that says "check this yourself."

In software terms, this is a type checker for reasoning. In logic terms, this is a proof verifier. In AI safety terms, this is a machine that can audit its own inference against constitutional constraints.

The Minimal PrologAI Kernel is the constitutional layer of PrologAI reasoning. Rung 13 makes that layer checkable from the inside.

The code is open. The reasoning is readable. The mind is growing.
