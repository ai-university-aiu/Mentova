Accomplishment 218: Object Tiling and Multi-Copy Layout (objcopy Pack, tc_*, Layer 177)

Author: D. R. Dison, Founder of AIU, Creator of PrologAI and Mentova,
ORCID 0009-0001-9246-5758, linkedin.com/in/d-r-dison/

PrologAI now has an objcopy pack - 14 tc_* predicates that generate multiple positioned copies of object terms in one call.

The pack can tile an object into a row, a column, or a full 2D grid. It can stamp copies at an explicit position list. It aligns and packs lists of objects with configurable gaps, spreads objects at fixed intervals, centers a shape within a canvas, and flips shapes horizontally within a frame.

41 of 41 unit tests pass.

Together with the canvas pack (which renders object lists to grids) and the objxf pack (which transforms individual objects), the objcopy pack completes PrologAI's object-level layout and rendering toolkit. A reasoner can now go from a single template object to a fully-rendered output grid in three steps: copy, arrange, render.

This is WP 198, Mentova's 218th accomplishment.
