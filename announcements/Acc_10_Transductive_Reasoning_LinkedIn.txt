Mentova now classifies without generalising. Rung 10 of 48 is accomplished.

There are two ways to classify something you have not seen before.

The first way is to induce a rule from past cases and apply it: "all animals with feathers and wings are birds — this new thing has feathers and wings — therefore it is a bird." That is inductive reasoning. Mentova accomplished that at Rung 2.

The second way is to skip the rule entirely. Find the most similar things you already know about. Let them vote. That is transductive reasoning — from the particular to the particular, with no generalisation in between.

Rung 10 gives Mentova k-Nearest Neighbours.

Describe a new entity: wings=yes, feathers=yes, beak=yes, flies=yes, size=medium. Ask Mentova what it is.

  answer(bird, just(knn(k(3),
                        neighbours([1-bird-c1, 1-bird-c2, 1-bird-c7]),
                        voted(bird))))

Three nearest known cases: c1, c2, c7. All birds. Distance from the new point: 1 feature difference each. Vote: 3 to 0 for bird. Classification: bird.

No rule was formed. The label comes entirely from similarity to known cases.

Now try: wings=no, feathers=no, beak=no, flies=no, size=large.

  answer(mammal, just(knn(k(3),
                          neighbours([0-mammal-c4, 1-mammal-c3, 1-mammal-c6]),
                          voted(mammal))))

c4 is an exact match (distance 0). The next two nearest are also mammals. Vote: 3 to 0. Label: mammal.

Every classification shows:
  — k (the neighbourhood size chosen)
  — Each neighbour with its case ID and Hamming distance
  — The voted label

A human can read the justification, spot a neighbour that seems wrong, add a corrective case to the library, and re-query. No retraining. No weights to adjust. Just add a case.

This is the difference between a glass-box classifier and a black-box one.

Transductive reasoning is useful when:
  — The rule is too complex to induce reliably
  — You only need to classify this specific new point
  — The case library is more reliable than any rule you could extract
  — You want to show the classification evidence to a human reviewer

Rung 2 induced a rule from cases. Rung 10 classifies without forming one. Both modes are available in Mentova. The right tool depends on the task.
