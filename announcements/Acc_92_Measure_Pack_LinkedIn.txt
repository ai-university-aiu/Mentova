Accomplishment 92: Mentova can now measure what it sees.

The new measure pack gives PrologAI fourteen geometric predicates for describing
cell regions and grids with mathematical precision.

Area. Bounding box. Perimeter. Diameter. Extent ratio. Aspect ratio.
Row span. Column span. Centroid. Radius. Interior count. Border count.
Color count.

Every result is a deterministic integer. No floating-point approximation.
No machine-learning guesswork. Pure symbolic geometry.

Here is what that looks like in practice:

A 3x3 filled square has area 9, perimeter 12, one interior cell, and eight
border cells.

A horizontal bar of three cells has aspect ratio 3:1, diameter 2, and a centroid
at its middle cell.

An L-shape with three cells has extent 3/4 - it fills three of the four cells
in its bounding box.

These are the kinds of measurements that let a cognitive system describe what
it is looking at before it tries to reason about it.

You cannot compare two shapes if you cannot first describe each shape.

The measure pack is Layer 51, work package 72 in the PrologAI platform.
