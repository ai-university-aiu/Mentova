Mentova now does science. Rung 26 of 48 is accomplished.

Science is not just collecting data. It is forming a hypothesis, testing it against data, and scoring the result. The hypothesis either survives the test or it does not.

Rung 26 gives Mentova a scientific discovery loop.

Hypothesis: most observations of canaries show yellow colour.

Test: look at the observation table. 47 yellow observations. 3 green. Total: 50.

Score: 47/50 = 0.94. Grade: confirmed.

  answer(result(0.94, confirmed, evidence(canary,yellow,47,of(50))),
         just(scientific_test(pattern(dominant_colour(canary,yellow)),
                               score(0.94), grade(confirmed))))

The hypothesis is confirmed with a score of 0.94. Evidence is named: 47 yellow observations out of 50.

Now test: canaries swim.

Score: near zero. Grade: not_supported.

The discovery loop:
  1. Propose a hypothesis from a template
  2. Test against the observation table
  3. Score by coverage (how well the data supports the hypothesis)
  4. Grade: confirmed (≥0.80), supported (≥0.60), weakly_supported (≥0.40), not_supported (<0.40)

This is the scientific method in a glass box. Every step named. Every score justified by raw observation counts.

Scientific reasoning is built on what came before: the observation table (Rung 7), statistical analysis (Rung 7), and hypothetical reasoning (Rung 20). Now those capabilities come together in a complete discovery loop.
