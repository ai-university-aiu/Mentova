Author: D. R. Dison, Founder of AIU (Artificial Intelligence University). Creator of PrologAI and Mentova. ORCID: 0009-0001-9246-5758. linkedin.com/in/d-r-dison/

You do not need to know programming to use Mentova.

Today we publish the Mentova Tutorial - a four-chapter introduction to the Mentova Synthetic Mind, written for learners with no prior knowledge of programming, logic, or artificial intelligence.

If you are curious about what a reasoning AI looks like on the inside - not the opaque output of a large language model, but transparent, step-by-step reasoning you can inspect - this tutorial is for you.

Mentova reasons in more than 48 different ways. The tutorial tours every one of them with a working example and a plain-language explanation of what that kind of reasoning does. You will learn how to ask Mentova a deductive question, a counterfactual question, a moral question, a Bayesian question - and how to read the answer, including the full justification that shows how Mentova arrived at it.

The four chapters cover:

• Chapter 1: What Mentova is, what it can do, and what it cannot (yet) do.

• Chapter 2: Starting a session. Installing the tools, running bin/mentova, reading your first result.

• Chapter 3: The Reasoning Ladder. All 48+ reasoning types with working examples.

• Chapter 4: Memory, growth, bodies, and protocols. How Mentova stores knowledge, measures its own development, inhabits game and robot environments, and communicates with other AI systems through four protocols (MCP, A2A, ACP, ANP).

The tutorial is available in two formats:

• Multi-file: docs/tutorial/ in the Mentova repository. Four chapter files.

• Monolithic: docs/Mentova_Tutorial.txt. All four chapters in one file, ready for PDF conversion.

Every capability described in this tutorial was demonstrated and documented before the tutorial was written. That is the Tutorial Track discipline: show it, prove it, then teach it.
