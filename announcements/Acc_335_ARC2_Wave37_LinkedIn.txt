THE RULE

Grid background BG (most frequent value) and divider Div (most frequent non-BG
value) partition the non-Div cells into 4-connected components via flood-fill.
Each component fills with concentric rectangular rings. Ring 0 is the outermost
layer; ring K is all cells at 4-connected BFS distance K from the ring-0 source
set. Single-cell non-BG non-Div markers at specific ring positions define a
color cycle that repeats across rings.

THE KEY INSIGHT

Ring-0 detection requires 8-connected Div-adjacency, not 4-connected. When a
Div wall forms an L-shape with a corner cell, cells diagonally adjacent to that
corner are inside the inner concavity of the L-shaped component. With only
4-connected Div-adjacency these cells are ring-1. But they are physically
adjacent to the Div wall corner, so they must be ring-0 for the ring distances
in the concavity region to be correct.

The fix: classify a cell as ring-0 if any of its 8 neighbours is Div (instead
of any of its 4 neighbours). BFS propagation stays 4-connected. This two-mode
connectivity approach - 8-connected for ring-0 boundary, 4-connected for BFS -
is the minimal change that handles L-shaped components without breaking the
rectangular ring structure in simpler components.

Only two new ring-0 cells are added in the entire task (cells at the two
concavity corners of the outer L-shaped component in pair 1). Pairs 2 and 3
have only rectangular components and are completely unaffected.

WHAT THIS PROVES

PrologAI can identify when two different connectivity modes are needed within
the same algorithm - one mode for boundary classification, a different mode for
distance propagation - and express this as a compact named rule that generalizes
correctly across all training pairs and the test case.
