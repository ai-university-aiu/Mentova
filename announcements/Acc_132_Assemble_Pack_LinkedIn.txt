PrologAI has a new cognitive capability: the assemble pack (Layer 91, Work
Package 112).

Existing packs know how to manipulate a single grid. The assemble pack knows
how to BUILD a larger grid from multiple pieces.

14 predicates (as_* prefix):

• as_hcat - join a list of grids side by side (different grids, not the same tile repeated)
• as_vcat - stack a list of grids vertically
• as_grid_of - assemble a 2D matrix of grids into one combined grid
• as_downscale - reduce each K x K block to one cell by majority vote (inverse of seek pack's upscale)
• as_border - surround a grid with a W-cell wide colored frame
• as_center_in - embed a grid at the floor center of a larger canvas
• as_quarter - extract one named quadrant (tl, tr, bl, br)
• as_flip_h_cat - concatenate a grid with its own left-right mirror
• as_flip_v_cat - stack a grid with its own top-bottom mirror
• as_zip_h - interleave columns from two same-size grids
• as_zip_v - interleave rows from two same-size grids
• as_paste - unconditionally paste a sub-grid at a given position
• as_mask_fill - replace cells wherever a mask grid is non-zero
• as_crop_to - crop or pad a grid to an exact target size

42 PLUnit (Prolog Unit Testing) acceptance tests pass.

These capabilities directly support the kinds of spatial construction tasks
that appear in ARC-AGI-2 (Abstract and Reasoning Corpus for Artificial
General Intelligence - Year 2): building symmetric patterns from halves,
assembling tiled outputs from distinct sub-grids, extracting and repositioning
quadrants, creating bordered patterns, and combining two grids by interleaving.
