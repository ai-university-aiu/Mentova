Accomplishment 88 - Mentova now understands spatial relationships between regions.

D. R. Dison, Founder of AIU, Creator of PrologAI and Mentova, ORCID 0009-0001-9246-5758, linkedin.com/in/d-r-dison/

PrologAI just crossed 66 work packages.

The new relation pack gives Mentova the vocabulary to describe how regions relate to each other in space.

rl_above and rl_below - is one region entirely above or below another?

rl_left_of and rl_right_of - is one region entirely to the left or right?

rl_adjacent - do the two regions touch (4-connected)?

rl_distance - what is the minimum Manhattan distance between them?

rl_contained_bbox - is the bounding box of one region inside the other?

rl_overlap and rl_disjoint - do they share cells, or not?

rl_same_row and rl_same_col - do they share a grid row or column?

rl_centroid - what is the integer center of a region?

rl_offset - what is the centroid-to-centroid offset between two regions?

rl_direction - in which direction (above/below/left/right) would you travel from one to the other?

This matters because:

ARC-AGI-2 tasks frequently require understanding relative positions before a transformation rule can be written. Now Mentova has the vocabulary.

Spatial descriptions like "object A is above and left of object B" can now be computed and composed with the rest of the cognitive stack.

The relation pack works with any list of r(R,C) cells - compatible with pf_flood_fill, sh_from_grid, gd_objects, and every other pack in the stack.

The relation pack is the 66th work package in PrologAI and Mentova's 88th accomplishment.
