Mentova now reads patterns from data. Rung 7 of 48 is accomplished.

Individual facts are not enough. A mind that holds "canary observation: yellow, count 47; canary observation: green, count 3" has two facts. A mind that can conclude "canary is overwhelmingly yellow (94%)" has extracted a pattern. That extraction is statistical reasoning.

Rung 7 gives Mentova statistical analysis over its observation table.

Ask which property dominates in canary observations:

  answer(dominant(canary, yellow, 47),
         just(dominant(canary, yellow, 47, out_of(50))))

47 out of 50 canary observations are yellow. Yellow is the dominant property. Justified: the raw count is shown alongside the total.

Ask about roses:

  answer(most_common(rose, red, 82),
         just(most_common(rose, red, 82, total(100), pct(82))))

82% of rose observations are red. The percentage is computed and returned alongside the justification.

Ask about penguins — do they swim or fly?

  answer(trend(penguin, swims, flies, more(swims)),
         just(trend(penguin, swims, 95, flies, 0, more(swims))))

Swims: 95 observations. Flies: 0 observations. Trend: more(swims). This is the pass criterion for Rung 7 — the reported trend must match the data. It does, exactly.

This connects directly to what was already known from Rung 17 (defeasible reasoning): penguins are the exception to "birds fly." The statistical table now confirms the same truth from a different direction — empirically, not just by rule.

Ask what fraction of eagle observations show hunting:

  answer(0.71, just(proportion(eagle, hunts, 71, 100, 0.71)))

71% of eagle observations involve hunting. The count, total, and proportion are all named in the justification.

Four query types now available:
  — dominant(Subject): find the most frequently observed property
  — most_common(Subject): dominant property with percentage
  — proportion(Subject, Property): fraction showing a given property
  — trend(Subject, PropA, PropB): which of two properties dominates
  — compare(S1, S2, Property): which subject shows higher rate

Every result names the raw counts so the reasoning can be audited against the data.

This rung is the statistical foundation for Rung 26, where Mentova will form and score scientific hypotheses. You cannot score a hypothesis without being able to read the data first.
