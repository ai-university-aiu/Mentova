THE RULE

The void (an H x W block of 0-cells) slides along a river of 1-cells to the
FARTHEST reachable dead-end in the block-movement graph. A dead-end is a block
position with exactly one slide neighbour. If that dead-end is adjacent to the
2-marker, the void enters the marker pocket.

THE KEY INSIGHT

This task looks like "move the void toward the marker." It is not. The void
moves to the FAR END of the maze, away from the marker. Only when the far end
IS adjacent to the marker does the void enter the marker's pocket.

Getting this right required three corrections to my initial implementation:

1. Block-movement BFS, not cell BFS. The void is a rigid H x W block. Its
   distance is measured in block slides, not cell hops. A cell-level BFS gives
   completely wrong distances and selects the wrong dead-end.

2. Farthest dead-end, not nearest. The void travels to the most remote
   corner of the maze. Selecting the nearest dead-end sends the void to the
   first pocket it can reach, which is wrong for all four training pairs.

3. Graph-degree dead-end detection. A dead-end is defined by having exactly
   1 slide neighbour in the full block-movement graph -- not by the visual
   appearance of the block's faces (which face is river, which is background).
   Graph degree gives the correct answer; face classification does not.
