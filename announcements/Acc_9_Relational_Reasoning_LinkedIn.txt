Mentova completes its birth sequence. Rung 9 of 48 is accomplished.

The first nine rungs are called the birth sequence of a Synthetic Mind in the PrologAI demonstration plan. Rung 9 — relational reasoning — is the last of them. Mentova is now minimum-viable.

What does relational reasoning add? The ability to answer questions that require traversing a chain of named connections across the knowledge graph.

Ask: starting from canary, follow two is_a edges — where do you end up?

  answer(animal, just(two_hop(canary, is_a, bird, is_a, animal)))

canary is_a bird. bird is_a animal. Two hops. Intermediate node named: bird. Result: animal. Every step shown.

Ask: follow three is_a edges from canary:

  answer(living_thing,
         just(three_hop(canary, is_a, bird, is_a, animal, is_a, living_thing)))

canary → bird → animal → living_thing. Three hops. The full chain is the justification.

Ask for the complete path from canary to living_thing:

  answer(yes, just(ancestor(canary, living_thing, chain([canary, bird, animal, living_thing]))))

The path [canary, bird, animal, living_thing] is returned. Every intermediate node named. You can trace every step.

Ask what canary and penguin have in common (by is_a):

  answer([bird], just(common(canary, penguin, is_a, [bird])))

Both are birds. One common is_a target: bird. Named and justified.

This is the difference between a lookup table and a reasoning system. A lookup table can tell you that canary is_a bird. A reasoning system can tell you that canary is_a bird is_a animal is_a living_thing — and show you every step.
