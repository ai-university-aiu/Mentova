Mentova now reasons about emotions. Rung 44 of 48 is accomplished.

Emotions are not arbitrary. They arise from how situations relate to what you care about. Win something you wanted? Joy. Face an uncertain threat? Fear. Receive an unexpected insult? Anger.

This is cognitive appraisal theory: emotions are derived from two dimensions — goal congruence (does this situation serve your goals or oppose them?) and certainty (do you know the outcome?).

Rung 44 gives Mentova emotional reasoning.

  Agent: alice. Situation: win_competition. Appraisal: goal_congruent, certain.
  Query: appraise(alice, win_competition)
  Answer: emotion(joy, high, approach)
  Justification: goal_congruent + certain → joy, intensity(high), tendency(approach)

  Agent: bob. Situation: threat_detected. Appraisal: goal_incongruent, uncertain.
  Query: how_might_feel(bob, threat_detected)
  Answer: emotion_profile(fear, high, negative)

Seven emotion types from two dimensions:
  goal_congruent + certain → joy (high, approach)
  goal_congruent + uncertain → hope (medium, approach)
  goal_incongruent + certain → sadness (high, withdrawal)
  goal_incongruent + uncertain → fear (high, avoidance)
  goal_congruent + certain, prosocial → gratitude (medium, prosocial)

Also: response tendencies. Joy → approach. Fear → avoidance. Anger → aggression.

Emotional reasoning is what empathetic AI needs. Understanding how a situation feels to someone — what emotion it would produce, what tendency it would drive — is the foundation of emotionally intelligent interaction.
