Accomplishment 127: The Step Pack - Directional Grid Movement

Mentova's 105th work package is live.

The step pack adds 14 st_* predicates for directional grid navigation: taking
one unbounded step in any direction (st_step), one bounded step that fails if
out of the grid (st_step_in), casting a ray to collect all in-bounds cells in
a direction excluding the start (st_ray), stopping a ray before a given value
(st_ray_to), walking in a direction including the start cell (st_walk), the
four cardinal directions (st_dirs4), all eight principal directions including
diagonals (st_dirs8), rotating a direction 90 degrees clockwise (st_rotate_cw)
or counter-clockwise (st_rotate_ccw), reversing a direction (st_opposite),
computing the unit step direction between two cells (st_normalize), following
a list of turns from a starting position (st_path), finding the first cell
in a direction with a given value (st_first), and counting the steps remaining
to the grid boundary (st_to_edge).

Direction is the first lens of spatial reasoning. Before the step pack, Mentova
had axis-aligned rays in the trace pack - but no general 8-directional movement,
no direction arithmetic, and no path following. The step pack closes that gap
and integrates cleanly with the walk, trace, window, and scene packs for
comprehensive spatial perception.

All 42 acceptance tests pass. The pack is documented in SPARC Specification
v72 (Part 97, ST-ST-001 through ST-ST-014), Refinement v120, and tutorial
Chapter 68.
