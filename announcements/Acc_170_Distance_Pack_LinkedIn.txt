Mentova Accomplishment 170: The Cell Distance and Proximity Computation Pack

PrologAI gains its 150th work package today.

The distance pack (Layer 129, dt_* prefix) provides 14 predicates for
computing distances between grid cells, building distance transform grids,
and performing proximity queries.

Three distance metrics are supported:

Manhattan (L1): sum of absolute row and column differences.
Chebyshev (L-infinity): maximum of absolute row and column differences,
equal to king-move count on a chess board.
Squared Euclidean: integer squared distance, no floating point.

The 14 predicates cover:

Basic distances: Manhattan (dt_manhattan), Chebyshev (dt_chebyshev),
and squared Euclidean (dt_euclidean_sq).

Nearest and farthest: nearest cell of a target value by Manhattan
distance with row-major tie-breaking (dt_nearest); farthest cell
(dt_farthest).

Cells at distance: all cells of a value at exactly D (dt_all_at);
all positions within Manhattan radius D (dt_within_manhattan);
all positions within Chebyshev radius D (dt_within_chebyshev).

Distance transforms: same-size grid where each cell holds its distance
to the nearest target-value cell, Manhattan variant (dt_map_manhattan)
and Chebyshev variant (dt_map_chebyshev).

Ring and centroid: positions at exactly D forming a ring shape
(dt_ring_manhattan); integer floor centroid of a value's cells
(dt_centroid).

Diameter: maximum pairwise Manhattan distance of a cell set with the
two farthest cells returned (dt_diameter_manhattan); Chebyshev variant
(dt_diameter_chebyshev).
