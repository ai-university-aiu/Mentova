PrologAI just crossed 60 work packages.

The new motion pack gives Mentova the ability to reason about where things go.

mv_gravity_down, mv_gravity_up, mv_gravity_left, mv_gravity_right - four gravity directions, each applied column-by-column or row-by-row.

mv_shift_grid - translate every cell in a grid by a (row, column) offset.

mv_scene_gravity - apply gravity to a scene of named objects, via a render-simulate-reparse cycle.

mv_distance - Manhattan distance between any two grid cells.

mv_closest_cell - nearest cell in a list to a reference point.

These 13 predicates complete the spatial reasoning layer: grid structure, object extraction, similarity measurement, counting, pattern detection, pipeline composition, and now motion.

Mentova can now describe a full visual scene - objects present, counts, spatial arrangement - and simulate what happens when gravity or displacement acts on it.

That is spatial intuition as a symbolic, verifiable, inspectable computation.

The motion pack is the 60th work package in PrologAI and Mentova's 82nd accomplishment.
