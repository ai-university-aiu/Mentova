Mentova now applies the law. Rung 47 of 48 is accomplished.

The law has rules. Rules have exceptions. Exceptions have their own cases. And cases establish precedents that future decisions must follow.

Rung 47 gives Mentova legal reasoning — rule application, exception checking, and precedent citation.

Scenario 1: bare fact.

  Scenario: ['person takes property belonging to another without consent']
  Rule: theft_rule (r1)
  Query: apply(r1, Scenario)
  Answer: verdict(liable(theft))

Theft rule applies. Verdict: liable.

Scenario 2: same fact plus an exception.

  Scenario: [..., 'property taken to prevent greater harm']
  Exception: necessity_defence
  Answer: verdict(exempt(necessity))

The exception overrides the rule. Exempt.

Precedent:

  Query: precedent(case_donoghue_stevenson)
  Answer: precedent(case_donoghue_stevenson, r4, liable(negligence),
                    'manufacturer owes duty of care to end consumer')

Donoghue v Stevenson. The ratio: manufacturer owes duty of care to end consumer. Rule r4 (negligence).

Five rules: theft, self_defence, contract, negligence, trespass.
Five exceptions: necessity, owner_consent, volenti, implied_licence, plus more.
Four precedents: Donoghue, Carlill, R v Hasan, Entick v Carrington.
