Rung 34 accomplished: Epistemic Reasoning — Mentova passes the Sally-Anne false-belief test.

The scenario: Sally places a marble in a basket and leaves. Anne moves the marble to a box. Sally returns.

Where does Sally think the marble is?

    mentova_query(epistemic, believes(sally, marble_in_basket), R)
    → answer(true, ...)

    mentova_query(epistemic, believes(anne, marble_in_basket), R)
    → answer(false, ...)

    mentova_query(epistemic, false_belief(sally, marble_in_basket), R)
    → false_belief(sally, marble_in_basket, their_belief(true), others_differ)
      note: "Agent holds belief others contradict"

Sally believes the marble is in the basket. Anne believes it is not. Mentova identifies that Sally holds a false belief — her belief contradicts another agent's.

This is the Sally-Anne test, originally developed in developmental psychology to check for theory of mind. Children with autism often fail it; typical children pass around age 4-5.

Mentova passes it now, at Rung 34 of 48.

A system that can model what another agent believes — including a belief that is false — can reason about deception, instruction, and collaboration in a way that goes well beyond pattern-matching.
