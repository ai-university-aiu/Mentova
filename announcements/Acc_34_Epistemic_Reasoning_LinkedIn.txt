Mentova now reasons about who knows what. Rung 34 of 48 is accomplished.

There is a difference between what is true and what an agent knows. There is a difference between what an agent knows and what an agent merely believes. There is a difference between what one agent knows and what is common knowledge among all agents.

Rung 34 gives Mentova epistemic reasoning.

  Query: knows(alice, canary_is_yellow)
  Answer: knows(alice, canary_is_yellow, 0.9)
  Justification: epistemic(knowledge(alice, canary_is_yellow), confidence(0.9))

Alice knows canary_is_yellow with confidence 0.9.

  Query: ignorant(bob, tweety_is_canary)
  Answer: ignorant(bob, tweety_is_canary)
  Justification: reason(no_knowledge_or_belief)

Bob has no knowledge or belief about tweety being a canary. He is ignorant of it.

  Query: common_knowledge(fire_is_hot)
  Answer: not_common(fire_is_hot)
  Justification: result(not_universally_known)

Fire being hot is not common knowledge — not all agents have it registered.

Three agents: mentor, alice, bob. Each with their own knowledge inventory. Each with beliefs from the Small-World KB. Epistemic queries work across all of them.

This is the foundation of theory-of-mind reasoning: understanding what other agents know, what they believe, and where their ignorance lies.
