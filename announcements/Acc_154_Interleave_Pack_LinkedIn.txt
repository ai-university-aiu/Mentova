PrologAI can now merge, split, weave, and stride grids by rows and columns.

Work Package 134 - the interleave pack - adds 14 il_* predicates for row and column interleaving. 42/42 tests.

The core operations:

Merge two grids by alternating rows: il_rows
Merge by alternating columns: il_cols
Split back apart: il_derows, il_decols

Weaving adds separators; unweaving removes them:

il_weave_rows inserts a background row between every pair of rows.
il_unweave_rows removes the separators to recover the original.
Same operations for columns: il_weave_cols, il_unweave_cols.

Stride selection picks every Nth row or column:

il_stride_rows([[r0],[r1],[r2],[r3]], 2, 0, S).  % S = [[r0],[r2]]
il_stride_cols([[1,2,3,4]], 2, 1, S).            % S = [[2,4]]

Two more tools:

il_rows_n: round-robin interleave from a list of grids.
For [G1, G2, G3] each with H rows: G1[0], G2[0], G3[0], G1[1], G2[1], G3[1], ...
The findall implementation: between (row index) iterated before member (grid) gives round-robin naturally.

il_block_rows: partition rows into fixed-height panels.
il_block_rows(Grid, 2, Bs) splits every 2 rows into its own sub-grid.
