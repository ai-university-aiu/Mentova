PrologAI just crossed 62 work packages.

The new path pack gives Mentova spatial connectivity reasoning.

pf_flood_fill finds all cells of a given color reachable from a seed.

pf_components finds every connected group of a color in the grid.

pf_largest_component picks the biggest one.

pf_is_connected tests whether all cells of a color form one region.

pf_shortest_path finds the BFS shortest route from start to goal, avoiding walls.

pf_reachable finds everywhere you can go from a seed without crossing walls.

These are classic graph traversal algorithms - BFS and connected components - applied to grid-based visual reasoning.

Now Mentova can answer:
How many separate blue regions are there?
Which red blob is largest?
Can I get from cell A to cell B without crossing black walls?
What is the bounding box of the leftmost object?

These questions come up constantly in visual inductive reasoning. Now they have named, composable, inspectable answers in pure symbolic Prolog.

The path pack is the 62nd work package in PrologAI and Mentova's 84th accomplishment.
