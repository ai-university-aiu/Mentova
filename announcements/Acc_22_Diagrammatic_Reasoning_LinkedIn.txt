Mentova now reads grids and interprets spatial patterns. Rung 22 of 48 is accomplished.

Not all knowledge is propositional. Sometimes knowledge is laid out in space — in a grid, a map, a matrix. Diagrammatic reasoning is the ability to read that spatial layout and answer questions about it.

Rung 22 gives Mentova a grid reader.

Given the cross-shaped grid:
  o x o
  x x x
  o x o

Ask what is at the centre:

  answer(x, just(diagrammatic(cross, centre_cell(x))))

The centre cell is x. Correct.

Ask how many x symbols appear:

  answer(5, just(diagrammatic(cross, symbol(x), count(5))))

Five. One at centre, four arms. Correct.

The grid is named, stored, and readable. The pattern is interpreted. The answer is justified.

This is the foundation for reasoning about matrices, maps, and visual puzzles — any domain where knowledge has spatial structure.
