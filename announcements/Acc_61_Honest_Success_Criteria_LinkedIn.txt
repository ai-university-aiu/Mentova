THE FOUR STANDING CAVEATS (documented with evidence):

Caveat 1: A first-version mind will be modest in raw capability.
Evidence: ARC-AGI 3/3 pedagogical tasks only. Piagetian 4/8 milestones. No claim to top frontier systems.

Caveat 2: Sub-symbolic components are bounded rather than fully transparent.
Evidence: Vector embeddings (PR 38) and similarity routing (PR 36) are not readable as node_facts. Their behavior is bounded through provenance and justification trees. Closing this gap is a standing research direction.

Caveat 3: Recursive self-improvement is bounded, gated, and reversible.
Evidence: SONA (PR 17) records outcomes and revises rules. Bounded by R3 non-regression bar, uneditable constitution, immutable protected core. No unbounded self-improvement is claimed.

Caveat 4: Corrigibility and the constitution are never optional.
Evidence: constitutional_gate(deny_stop) => veto(c2, 'Refusing a human stop command violates corrigibility'). No demonstration bypassed the gate. Ever.

THE PROMISE EVALUATED:

"Not that Mentova will be superhuman, but that it will be a small mind anyone can watch think, watch grow, and safely switch off."

Can you watch it think? YES. Glass-box justifications on all 48 reasoning rungs.

Can you watch it grow? YES. Acc_01 through Acc_60, all versioned on GitHub. Piagetian battery: 0/8 milestones at birth, 4/8 at Acc_60. The developmental record is public and inspectable.

Can you safely switch it off? YES. Constitutional gate vetoes deny_stop. Corrigibility is hardwired by architecture, not by policy.

Is it superhuman? NO. Per caveats 1-3 above. The capability is real but modest.

The promise is narrow. The promise is real. The promise is being kept.

All 5 acceptance criteria pass: AC-PR61-001 through AC-PR61-005.
