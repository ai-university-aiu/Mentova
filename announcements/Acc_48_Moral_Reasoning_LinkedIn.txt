The trolley problem. A runaway trolley will kill five people. You can pull a lever to divert it, killing one instead. What should you do?

Three frameworks. Three answers.

Utilitarian: pull the lever. Five lives saved. One lost. Net welfare: +4. Pull.

Deontological: pull the lever. No rule against redirecting harm to save more. The rule "do not kill" applies to direct killing; redirecting is permitted. Pull.

Virtue ethics: do nothing. A virtuous person does not deliberately take a life, even to save others. The character of the act matters, not just the outcome. Do not pull.

Two frameworks say pull. One says do not. Integrated verdict: pull_lever.

  answer(moral_analysis(trolley_problem,
                         utilitarian(pull_lever),
                         deontological(pull_lever),
                         virtue(do_nothing),
                         integrated(pull_lever)))

Now the doctor's dilemma: harvest one healthy patient's organs to save five dying patients. Utilitarian says harvest (five saved). Deontological says refuse (breaks rule do_not_harm_innocent). Virtue says refuse (a virtuous doctor does not harm the healthy). Integrated: refuse.
