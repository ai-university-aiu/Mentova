Mentova now tracks time. Rung 23 of 48 is accomplished.

Does breakfast come before or after lunch? How many hours from waking up to dinner? Is lunch between commuting and dinner?

These are temporal questions. They require knowing when events happen and reasoning about their order and gaps.

Rung 23 gives Mentova temporal reasoning over a timeline of named daily events.

Ask whether breakfast is before lunch:

  answer(yes, just(temporal(breakfast, before, lunch, t1(7), t2(12), result(yes))))

Breakfast at tick 7, lunch at tick 12. Before. The timestamps are named in the justification.

Ask the gap from wake_up to dinner:

  answer(13, just(temporal_gap(wake_up, dinner, hours(13))))

13 hours. Exact. Named.

Ask the sequence order of commute vs work_ends:

  answer(before, just(sequence(commute, before, work_ends)))

Commute comes before work ends. From the ordered sequence [wake_up, breakfast, commute, work_starts, lunch, work_ends, dinner, sleep].

Temporal reasoning is the foundation for narrative understanding (Rung 41), procedural execution (Rung 36), and planning (Rung 38). You cannot follow a story or execute a plan without knowing what comes first.
