Mentova now knows what drives people. Rung 45 of 48 is accomplished.

Not all needs are equal. Hungry people do not think about esteem. People in danger do not think about self-actualisation. Lower-level needs must be satisfied first.

Rung 45 gives Mentova motivational reasoning — a need hierarchy and drive registry for understanding what motivates an agent right now.

  Query: urgent_need(alice)
  Answer: need(4, esteem, 'respect, achievement, recognition, status')

Alice's physiological (1), safety (2), and belonging (3) needs are satisfied. Her most urgent unsatisfied need is esteem — level 4.

  Query: urgent_need(mentor)
  Answer: all_satisfied(mentor)

The mentor has all five levels satisfied. No urgent unsatisfied need.

  Query: all_drives(bob)
  Answer: drives(bob, [find_safety-high, make_friends-high, earn_income-high])

Bob's three drives are all high urgency: find_safety, make_friends, earn_income. That makes sense — his safety (level 2) is unsatisfied.

Five need levels: physiological (1), safety (2), belonging (3), esteem (4), self_actualise (5).

Three agents: alice (levels 1-3 satisfied), bob (only level 1 satisfied), mentor (all satisfied).

Motivational reasoning is what personalisation engines, coaching tools, and empathetic AI need. Understand what someone needs most right now — and you understand what they will act on.
