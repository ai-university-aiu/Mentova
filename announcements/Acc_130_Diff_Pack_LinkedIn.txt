PrologAI gains the diff pack - 14 predicates for analyzing what changes
between grids, both within a single training pair and across all training
pairs at once.

What the diff pack provides:

Single-pair cell analysis:
• df_cell_diff - all changed cells as diff(R,C,OldValue,NewValue) terms
• df_added - cells that went from background to non-background
• df_removed - cells that went from non-background to background
• df_recolored - cells that stayed non-background but changed color
• df_stable - cells whose value did not change
• df_palette_change - colors added to or lost from the palette

Multi-pair contrastive analysis:
• df_common_diffs - cells that changed in EVERY training pair
• df_common_stable - cells that were stable in EVERY training pair
• df_always_added - cells added (BG to non-BG) in EVERY pair
• df_always_removed - cells removed (non-BG to BG) in EVERY pair

Diff manipulation:
• df_total_changes - total count of changed cells
• df_apply_diffs - apply a diff list to a grid
• df_invert_diffs - reverse each diff to reconstruct the original
• df_filter_diffs - keep only diffs matching a goal

Why multi-pair contrastive analysis?

Rule induction from grid tasks requires understanding what is INVARIANT
across all training examples.

df_common_diffs finds cells that changed in every pair - these are almost
certainly the transformation targets.

df_common_stable finds cells that never changed in any pair - these are
likely the background structure the rule leaves untouched.

This cross-pair intersection analysis is the symbolic equivalent of what
human puzzle solvers do when they compare multiple examples before forming
their hypothesis.
