Rung 33 accomplished: Modal Reasoning — Mentova now distinguishes what must be true from what merely might be.

Three propositions, three verdicts:

    birds_fly   → necessary (true in all four accessible worlds)
    fire_is_hot → necessary (true in all four worlds)
    tweety_flies → contingent (true in w1, w2; false in actual and w3)

The world model: actual, w1, w2, w3. Tweety is a penguin — in the actual world and w3, tweety does not fly. In w1 and w2 (hypothetical worlds where tweety has no flightless property), tweety does fly. So tweety_flies is possible (witness: w1) but not necessary.

    mentova_query(modal, classify(tweety_flies), R)
    → answer(contingent, just(modal(classify(tweety_flies), classification(contingent))))

This is why modal logic matters: it lets you say "this is necessarily so" vs "this happens to be so" — a distinction that matters enormously for planning, law, and science.
