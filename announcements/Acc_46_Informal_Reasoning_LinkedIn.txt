Mentova now detects fallacies. Rung 46 of 48 is accomplished.

"Your argument is wrong because you are inexperienced." That is not a refutation. That is an ad hominem — attacking the person instead of the argument.

"All swans I have seen are white, therefore all swans are white." That is a hasty generalisation — drawing a broad conclusion from too few examples.

"I ate an apple, then I got sick. The apple caused it." That is a post hoc fallacy — assuming causation from sequence.

Rung 46 gives Mentova informal reasoning — the ability to detect logical fallacies and score argument plausibility.

  Query: detect_fallacy(arg1)
  Answer: fallacy(arg1, ad_hominem, 'attacks the person not the argument')

  Query: detect_fallacy(arg5)
  Answer: fallacy(arg5, hasty_generalisation, 'draws broad conclusion from few examples')

  Query: plausibility(arg4)
  Answer: plausibility(arg4, 0.2, implausible)

Post hoc argument: 0.2 plausibility. Implausible.

Eight fallacy types: ad_hominem, straw_man, false_dilemma, slippery_slope, appeal_to_authority, circular, hasty_generalisation, post_hoc. All with explanations.

Also: rhetorical move identification (analogy, concession, rebuttal, modus_ponens) and Gricean implicature reasoning ("It is not bad" implies "Speaker thinks it is quite good").

Informal reasoning is what you need to navigate everyday argument. It is the difference between being persuaded by rhetoric and being persuaded by evidence.
