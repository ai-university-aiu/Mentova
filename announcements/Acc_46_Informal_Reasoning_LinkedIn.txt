MENTOVA DETECTS THE FLAW IN AN ARGUMENT — Rung 46 Accomplished

Author: D. R. Dison, Founder of AIU (Artificial Intelligence University). Creator of PrologAI and Mentova. Open Researcher and Contributor ID (ORCID): 0009-0001-9246-5758. (https://www.linkedin.com/in/d-r-dison/)


I am pleased to announce the accomplishment of Rung 46 of the Mentova reasoning ladder: Informal Reasoning.

Mentova can now detect logical fallacies by name, type, and explanation — the foundation of critical thinking and adversarial reasoning.

Demonstrated live:

    mentova_query(informal, detect_fallacy(arg1), R)
    → fallacy(arg1, ad_hominem, 'attacks the person not the argument')

    mentova_query(informal, detect_fallacy(arg2), R)
    → fallacy(arg2, straw_man, 'misrepresents the opponent\'s position')

    mentova_query(informal, detect_fallacy(arg3), R)
    → fallacy(arg3, false_dilemma, 'presents only two options when more exist')

    mentova_query(informal, detect_fallacy(arg4), R)
    → fallacy(arg4, slippery_slope, 'assumes one step leads inevitably to extreme outcome')

A valid-seeming argument is not a sound argument. Mentova can now tell the difference.
