Accomplishment 85 - Mentova now recognizes symmetry and computes canonical shapes.

D. R. Dison, Founder of AIU, Creator of PrologAI and Mentova, ORCID 0009-0001-9246-5758, linkedin.com/in/d-r-dison/

PrologAI just crossed 63 work packages.

The new symmetry pack gives Mentova the D4 symmetry group as first-class predicates.

sy_is_hsymmetric, sy_is_vsymmetric, sy_is_rot180 - three reflection and rotation tests.

sy_is_rot90, sy_is_diagonal, sy_is_antidiagonal - three more for square grids.

sy_group - returns the complete list of symmetry atoms that hold for a grid.

sy_orbit - all distinct orientations of a grid under 8 possible transforms.

sy_canonical - the lexicographically smallest orientation; the same for all rotations and reflections of the same shape.

sy_equivalent - tells you if two grids are the same shape in different orientations.

sy_order - tells you how symmetric a grid is: 1 (none) to 8 (fully symmetric).

This matters because:

Two L-shaped objects that are rotated 90 degrees are the same shape. Now Mentova knows that.

A checkerboard pattern is fully symmetric. Now Mentova can prove that in one call.

Finding the canonical form of a shape is a prerequisite for shape-based induction. Now Mentova has it.

The symmetry pack is the 63rd work package in PrologAI and Mentova's 85th accomplishment.
