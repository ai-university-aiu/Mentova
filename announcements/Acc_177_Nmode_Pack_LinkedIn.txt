Mentova Accomplishment 177: The Neighborhood Mode Filter Pack

PrologAI gains its 157th work package today.

The nmode pack (Layer 136, nm_* prefix) provides 14 predicates for
computing the mode (most frequent value) of lists, grid rows, columns,
the entire grid, and per-cell 4-connected and 8-connected neighborhoods.

The mode is the value that appears most frequently. When values are tied,
the smallest tied value wins as a deterministic tie-breaker.

This completes the statistical neighborhood triad:
- naggr (Layer 134): sum, max, min, mean, range, spread, diff count per cell
- median (Layer 135): middle value for lists, rows, columns, grid, filter
- nmode (Layer 136): most frequent value, uniform detection, outlier detection

The 14 predicates cover:

Single-list: nm_mode (the mode), nm_mode_all (all tied values), nm_mode_count
(mode with its frequency).

Per-row and per-column: nm_row, nm_col, nm_row_modes, nm_col_modes.

Grid-wide: nm_grid.

Mode filters: nm_filter4 and nm_filter8 replace each cell with the mode
of the cell and its in-bounds 4 or 8 neighbors - smoothing patterns while
preserving the dominant local value. A checkerboard grid under nm_filter8
collapses to a uniform grid because every cell has equal numbers of both
alternating values, and the tie goes to the smallest.

Uniform detection: nm_uniform4 and nm_uniform8 find cells whose
in-bounds neighbors all share one value.

Outlier detection: nm_outlier4 and nm_outlier8 find cells whose own value
disagrees with the mode of their neighbors (cell excluded from the mode).

Implementation detail: a cut in the empty-list base case of nm_count_prefix_/4
prevents duplicate solutions when nm_mode is called inside findall.
