PrologAI now has a scenerank pack - 14 rk_* predicates that rank candidate
symbolic rule terms by coverage across Before-After scene pair training examples.

Coverage: how many pairs does a given rule correctly explain? rk_coverage counts
them. rk_coverage_pairs lists them. rk_consistent checks all-or-nothing. The
pack handles the full sceneapply rule vocabulary (recolor, shift, color_map,
remove_color, reflect, and more) through a self-contained rk_apply_ engine.

Ranking: rk_rank_rules sorts candidates by coverage descending (negated-count
msort, tie-broken by term order). rk_best_rule picks the winner.
rk_rank_candidates returns Coverage-Rule pairs for display.
rk_top_n_rules returns the top N candidates.

Filtering: rk_all_consistent, rk_filter_min_coverage, rk_perfect_rules
(coverage == pair count) all use include/3 with encapsulated helper predicates.

With ruleinfer inferring candidates and scenerank selecting the best one,
PrologAI now has a complete infer-rank-apply pipeline.
