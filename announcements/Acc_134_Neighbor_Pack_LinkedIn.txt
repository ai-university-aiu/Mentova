PrologAI has a new cognitive capability: the neighbor pack (Layer 93, Work
Package 114).

Many visual reasoning tasks require knowing what is IMMEDIATELY adjacent
to a cell: what colors surround it, whether it is on the edge or interior
of a shape, whether two shapes are touching. The neighbor pack answers
those questions directly.

14 predicates (nb_* prefix):

• nb_4neighbors - list of nb(Row,Col,Val) terms for each valid 4-connected neighbor
• nb_8neighbors - list of nb(Row,Col,Val) terms for each valid 8-connected neighbor (including diagonals)
• nb_is_boundary - test: non-Bg cell with at least one Bg or out-of-bounds 4-neighbor
• nb_is_interior - test: non-Bg cell where all 4-neighbors are in-bounds and non-Bg
• nb_boundary_cells - sorted R-C pairs of all boundary cells in a grid
• nb_interior_cells - sorted R-C pairs of all interior cells in a grid
• nb_count_same - count of 4-neighbors with the same color as the cell
• nb_count_diff - count of 4-neighbors with a different color from the cell
• nb_adjacent_colors - sorted set of distinct colors among 4-neighbors
• nb_contour - sorted R-C pairs of all Color cells adjacent to non-Color or grid boundary
• nb_color_touches - succeed if any cell of ColorA is 4-adjacent to any cell of ColorB
• nb_touching_pairs - sorted (R1-C1)-(R2-C2) pairs of all ColorA-ColorB 4-adjacencies
• nb_flood_fill - replace the 4-connected region from a seed with a fill value, returning the modified grid
• nb_dilate - expand a Color region by one layer by converting adjacent Bg cells to Color

42 PLUnit (Prolog Unit Testing) acceptance tests pass.

The connect pack (Layer 64) returns cell lists from flood fill, not a modified
grid, and its border/interior predicates take a pre-existing region as input.
The neighbor pack works directly with a grid and a background color.
These are complementary, not duplicates.
