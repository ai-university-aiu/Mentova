Accomplishment 93: Mentova can now transform what it sees.

The new transform pack gives PrologAI fourteen predicates for applying spatial
and color transformations to entire grids.

Scale up. Scale down. Tile horizontally. Tile vertically. Transpose.
Flip left-right. Flip top-bottom. Rotate 90 degrees. Rotate 180 degrees.
Shift with fill. Apply a color map. Replace a color. Apply a mask.

Every result is deterministic.

No floating-point errors. No pixel rounding. Pure symbolic transformation.

Here is what that means in practice:

A grid scaled up by 3 produces a result where each original cell becomes a 3x3
block. If cell (1, 2) was color 5, every cell in the 3x3 block at (3..5, 6..8)
of the output is also color 5.

A grid shifted right by 2 with fill color 0 slides every column 2 positions to
the right. The leftmost 2 columns are filled with 0. The rightmost 2 columns of
the original are lost.

A color map [0-9, 1-0] swaps color 0 and color 1 everywhere in the grid.

These are the building blocks of visual transformation rules.

Once you can name them, you can combine them.

Once you can combine them, you can solve problems that require multi-step
transformation pipelines.

The transform pack is Layer 52, work package 73 in the PrologAI platform.
