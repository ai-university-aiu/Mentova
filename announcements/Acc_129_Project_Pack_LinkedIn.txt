Shadow casting: a non-BG cell projects its value through empty (BG) cells in
one direction, stopping at the next non-BG cell or the grid boundary.

Axis projection: summarize a 2D grid into a 1D row or column profile, or
identify which rows/columns contain non-BG content.

• pj_shadow_right - rightward

• pj_col_last - row of bottommost non-BG in a column
• pj_row_first - column of leftmost non-BG in a row

Why shadow casting?

Shadow casting is distinct from gravity (gv_* pack): gravity MOVES cells to one
end of their axis. Shadow casting LEAVES cells in place and paints the empty
space beyond them with the source cell's color.

This models tasks where objects cast color "beams" or "rays" in a direction
through otherwise empty grid space.

Why axis projection?

Many grid reasoning tasks require reading the structure of a grid along one
dimension before applying a rule. Knowing which rows are occupied, how many
objects are in each column, or finding where an object begins in a row - these
are all axis-projection operations.
