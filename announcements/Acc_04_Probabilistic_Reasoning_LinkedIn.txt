Mentova now reasons under uncertainty. Rung 4 of 48 is accomplished.

For the first three rungs, Mentova reasoned in a world of certainties — facts were true or false, rules fired or did not. That is not how the world works. Rain does not certainly fall. Illness does not certainly cause fatigue. Every real-world proposition comes with a degree of likelihood, and a mind that cannot represent that degree cannot act wisely in the world.

Rung 4 changes that.

Ask Mentova the probability that it rains today:

  answer(0.3, just(weighted_fact(rain)))

Not just a number — a justification. The 0.3 came from a named weighted fact in the knowledge base. Auditable. Traceable. Correctable.

Ask it the probability that both rain and exercise occur:

  answer(0.21, just(conjunction(just(direct(rain,0.3)), just(direct(exercise,0.7)))))

0.3 × 0.7 = 0.21. The independence assumption is named. The leaves are named. The rule is named.

Ask it the probability that rain or a sprinkler caused the wet ground:

  answer(0.72, just(disjunction(just(direct(rain,0.3)), just(direct(sprinkler,0.6)))))

Inclusion-exclusion: 0.3 + 0.6 − 0.18 = 0.72. Exact. Explained.

The engine supports:
  — Direct weighted-fact lookup
  — Conjunction under independence (P(A∧B) = P(A)×P(B))
  — Disjunction by inclusion-exclusion (P(A∨B) = P(A)+P(B)−P(A)P(B))
  — Complement (P(¬A) = 1−P(A))
  — Conditional probability (P(A|B) = P(A∧B)/P(B))

Every result carries a structured justification term. No black-box estimate. No silent approximation. A glass-box probability algebra running on named facts.

This is the foundation for Rung 5, where Mentova will update its beliefs on new evidence — Bayesian reasoning. The probabilistic algebra here is the arithmetic substrate; Bayes is the epistemic update rule.
