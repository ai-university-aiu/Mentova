Mentova now reasons in all three classical directions. Rung 3: abduction — inference to the best explanation.

The ground is wet. Why?

Two possibilities in Mentova's knowledge base: it rained (prior probability 0.30) or the sprinkler was running (prior probability 0.60).

Mentova's answer:

    Best explanation: sprinkler (score 0.60)
    Evidence: cause(sprinkler, wet_ground), prior(0.60)
    Runner-up: rain (score 0.30)

The sprinkler wins. Not because rain is impossible — it's entirely possible — but because the sprinkler is more probable given what Mentova knows. And it shows you the full evidence record: which cause, which prior, which likelihood, which note. Every step readable.

This is what a good abductive reasoner does: it produces the counter-intuitive result when the evidence demands it. Rain feels like the obvious answer. The numbers say otherwise.
