PrologAI now has a scenecmp pack - 14 sm_* predicates for comparing two scenes
(Before and After obj(Color, Cells) lists) at the inventory level.

When analyzing a visual transformation, the first questions to ask are often the
simplest: did the number of objects change? Did new colors appear? Did the shapes
change? The scenecmp pack answers all of these without requiring an explicit
object-to-object matching.

It provides count comparisons (sm_same_n_objs, sm_same_total_cells), color comparisons
(sm_same_colors, sm_added_colors, sm_removed_colors), form comparisons (sm_same_forms,
sm_added_forms, sm_removed_forms), a color change magnitude (sm_n_color_change), and a
top-level change detector (sm_any_change) that short-circuits as soon as any difference
is found.

sm_forms uses normalized shapes: each object is translated to the origin and its cells
are sorted. Two objects have the same form regardless of position or color.
