Mentova now knows where things are. Rung 21 of 48 is accomplished.

The cat is on the mat. The mat is in the kitchen. The kitchen is in the house.

Where is the cat?

For a human, this is trivial. For a reasoning system, it requires transitive containment resolution — following a chain of in/on relations until you reach a root container.

Rung 21 gives Mentova spatial reasoning with reference frames.

Ask where the cat is:

  answer(chain(cat, [cat, mat, kitchen, house]),
         just(containment_chain(cat, [cat, mat, kitchen, house])))

The full containment chain: cat → mat → kitchen → house. Every step named.

Ask whether the cat is in the house:

  answer(yes, just(transitive_in(cat, house, yes, chain([cat, mat, kitchen, house]))))

Yes — transitively. The chain that proves it is shown.

Spatial reasoning is essential for robot navigation, scene understanding, and natural language queries about location. "Where did I leave my keys?" requires this kind of containment resolution.
