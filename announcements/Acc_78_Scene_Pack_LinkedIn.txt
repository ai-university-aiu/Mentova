MENTOVA ACC_78: THE SCENE PACK - OBJECT-CENTRIC REASONING FOR ARC-AGI-2

Mentova now sees objects, not just grids.

The scene pack (PR 58, Layer 37) gives Mentova a structured inventory of every object in a visual scene. Not a neural feature map. Not an embedding vector. A named, inspectable Prolog term.

    obj(2, [r(1,1), r(1,2), r(2,1)])

That is a blue L-shaped object. Three cells. Bounding box: r(1,1) to r(2,2). Normalized shape: [r(0,0), r(0,1), r(1,0)]. Centroid: r(1,1).

Every property is computable. Every computation is readable.

What the scene pack gives Mentova:

sc_bg_color/2 - identify the background color (most frequent color in the grid).

sc_objects/2 - extract all foreground objects: one obj(Color, Cells) per 4-connected component per color.

sc_grid_to_scene/2 - build a complete scene(Rows, Cols, BgColor, Objects) model.

sc_obj_shape/2 - normalized cell set: position-independent shape representation. Two objects have the same shape if and only if their normalized forms are identical.

sc_cells_touching/2 - are any cells of two objects 4-connected neighbors?

sc_contained_in/2 - is one object's bounding box inside another's?

sc_above/2, sc_below/2, sc_left_of/2, sc_right_of/2 - spatial ordering by bounding box.

sc_same_shape/2 - shape identity under normalization.

sc_largest/2, sc_smallest/2, sc_sort_by_size/3 - size ordering.

sc_count/2, sc_count_of_color/3 - counting.

44 PLUnit tests. All pass.

Why this matters for ARC-AGI-2:

Many ARC-AGI-2 tasks are not about transforming a grid. They are about reasoning over the objects in a grid: "find the two objects with the same shape", "move the largest object to the left of the smallest", "which object is inside the frame?"

The grid pack (Acc_76) perceives the grid. The analogy pack (Acc_77) infers whole-grid transformation rules. The scene pack (Acc_78) reasons about individual objects.

Three layers. Three packs. One glass-box substrate.
