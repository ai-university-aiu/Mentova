THE TASK

Each input grid contains colored objects and a 2-column legend in the lower-left.
The legend maps object color K to fill color V. The rule fills the bounding box
of each K-color's cells with V. When bounding boxes overlap, the smaller (tighter)
box's fill takes priority.

THE CREATIVE INSIGHT

Three fill modes coexist in a single legend:

- Standard fill: V is a normal color. Fill the bbox, preserve K-cells (sparse)
  or convert them to V (dense).

- Background fill (V = BG): Fill the bbox with the background color. This
  "punches through" any larger fill beneath it - essentially carving a hole in
  the bigger box's fill region.

- Erase (V = 0, BG != 0): No fill drawn. K-cells become transparent, letting
  whatever fill exists in an enclosing box show through.

The punch-through case (pair 1) was the most subtle: color-1 maps to 0 (= BG
in this grid), creating a hole inside the larger color-2 fill region. This
required detecting that V=0 means "fill with BG" (not erase) when BG is also 0.

THE ELEGANT TRICK

Instead of a multi-pass paint algorithm, all bounding boxes are sorted by area
ascending. The fill_for predicate scans this sorted list and returns the first
matching fill. Since smaller boxes come first, they automatically override larger
ones - no explicit priority management needed.
