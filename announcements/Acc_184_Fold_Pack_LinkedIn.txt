PrologAI gains its 164th work package: the fold pack (fd_*, Layer 143).

14 predicates for paper-origami reasoning on 2D grids.

fd_split_h splits a grid into top and bottom halves at a horizontal crease row.
fd_split_v splits into left and right halves at a vertical crease column.

fd_overlay combines two same-size grids: where grid A has a non-background cell,
A wins; where A has background, B shows through.

fd_fold_h folds the bottom half upward. Row I of the output overlays Top[I]
with Bottom[R-I]. Rows that have no bottom counterpart pass through unchanged.

fd_fold_v folds the right half leftward with the same mirror logic per column.

fd_unfold_h creates a symmetric full grid from a half by stacking it above its
upside-down copy. fd_unfold_v creates a symmetric grid by joining each row with
its own left-right reverse.

fd_sym_h tests whether a grid is symmetric under a fold crease after row R,
using the between-rows mirror formula: row I maps to row 2R+1-I. This formula
is consistent with fd_fold_h and avoids vacuous symmetry at R=0.

fd_sym_v is the column analogue.

fd_find_fold_h and fd_find_fold_v search for the first valid crease.

The roundtrip holds: unfold a half, then find-fold, and you recover the
original crease index.

fd_mark_row finds the first row where every cell equals a marker value.
fd_mark_col finds the first column where every cell equals a marker value.

fd_fold_both applies a horizontal fold then a vertical fold in one call,
reducing a grid to its top-left quadrant.
