Accomplishment 99: Mentova can now reason about where things are.

The new spatial pack gives PrologAI fourteen predicates for directional
and positional reasoning.

Is this cell north, south, east, or west of that one?
Exactly which of the nine compass directions separates them?
How many Manhattan steps apart?
How many Chebyshev king-moves apart?
What are the 4-connected neighbors within grid bounds?
What are the 8-connected neighbors?
Are these two cells adjacent?
Is this cell inside this bounding box?
Which cells in this region fall between row 2 and row 5?
Which cell in this list is closest to the reference point?
Which is farthest?
What is the integer centroid of this region?

Every answer is deterministic. Every answer is exact.

Here is a concrete example.

An ARC-AGI-2 task shows two objects. The rule: the smaller object
moves toward the larger object by one step.

Step 1: Find the direction.

sp_direction(SmallCenter, LargeCenter, Dir).
% Dir = northeast

Step 2: Find the adjacent neighbor in that direction.

sp_neighbors8(SmallCell, Rows-Cols, Neighbors),
member(Neighbor, Neighbors),
sp_direction(SmallCell, Neighbor, northeast).

The object moves one step northeast. Deterministic. Glass-box.
